King County doesn't exist on any maps, but you'll find it if you look hard enough -- tucked into a forgotten corner of Colorado's Western Slop, out along I-70 near the Utah border.

Then again, if you're reading this, you've already found it..

Out here, gas stations serve as town halls, the library smells like old paper and forgotten dreams, and the half-lit hospital halls hold more ghosts than gurneys.  Across from a neon-and-chrome truck stop, there's a dive bar where the jukebox is stuck playing Hank Williams and Johnny Cash, the regulars have short tempers and long memories, but the beer is always cold.

They say there's a barn out by the creek that remembers what you tried to forget. Words whispered before sunrise might escape -- sometimes forgotten, sometimes something entirely different.

They say the lawyer in the RV used to be someone. Made the news. Made a list, too. Now they say he's not allowed near schools..

They say if you follow the dirt road past the plow line, past the last road sign and sagging fences, you'll find a trailer park where the school bus doesn't stop anymore.  Nobody says why, though.

Kids go missing here.
Sometimes they come back.
Sometimes they never were to begin with.

Welcome to King County.
