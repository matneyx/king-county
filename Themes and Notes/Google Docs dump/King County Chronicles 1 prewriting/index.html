    Actual plot:

    • Copycat killer
    • Original case
      • This was the case that granted Shepherd Inn its diplomatic immunity.
      • The County Executive’s son and girlfriend were kidnapped
      • Son is a Vet of the Gulf War, like Rudy. Pregnant fiance, planning on getting married after the baby is born.
      • Executive receives, in an envelope, son’s dog tags and fiance’s engagement ring in an envelope with instructions to bring money to a location, and to come alone.
      • First attempt didn’t go so well -- the Executive brought cops, and the Kidnapper never showed. The next day, Executive received another envelope, this time with a severed finger and another note that said, “I said come alone.”
      • Executive approaches Rudy -- Exec approached a psychic, who said the only chance of getting his kids back was through Rudy.
      • Reference to Shepherd -- Christ analog?
      • Rudy accepts the case -- he’s new in town, still an active Hunter, and feels a kinship with the vic due to their time in the military. He takes the tags/ring/finger from the Exec.
      • Initial hunting is slow going -- he goes to the same psychic, who can’t foretell him anything. Instead, she refers him to a Chinese food restaurant, but gives no clues.
      • Chinese restaurant initially turns up nothing -- he goes, he eats, he mentions the psychic to the waitress, and the waitress says she doesn’t understand. He leaves, leaving no information.
      • A day passes and he’s at a standstill. That evening, while he’s fixing something immediate in the Inn, there’s a knock at the door and it’s a Chinese food delivery driver. Rudy says he didn’t order anything, the driver says it’s already paid for so rudy accepts -- it’s a simple General Tso's Chicken combo -- and when Rudy cracks open the fortune cookie there’s a handwritten note that has the address of the restaurant on one side, and “Midnight, tonight -- bring the finger.” on the other.
      • Rudy goes to the restaurant -- it’s closed, and after knocking on the front door and receiving no answer, he decides to go to the back. An older asian man opens and lets him in. Rudy asks some questions, the asian man answers them without giving much away about who he is or what he does while the man clears a table for them in the dining area of the restaurant -- behind a screen, out of view from the street. The asian man lays a bowl (Chinese Hot Pot) on the table and places a small wooden box next to it. He pours a shot of Baijiu into the bowl from a ceramic bottle, then opens the box and takes out herbs and such and sprinkles them into the bowl as Rudy sits in silence. The asian man asks for the finger, and when Rudy produces it, he drops it irreverently into the bowl, then asks for Rudy’s hand. Rudy reluctantly gives it, his right, and the man cuts deeply into Rudy’s palm then squeezes his blood into the bowl, chanting something as he does so. After a few moments of chanting, while the hot pot heats the saki, blood, and herbs, the man ladles the concoction back into an opaque shot glass and asks Rudy to drink. Rudy is hesitant, but the man says “While it’s still warm -- it’ll work cold, yes, but it’s just awful.” Rudy drinks.
      • Rudy is overcome with a vision Insert vision here (part of the vision includes instructing the father to do as the note asks and go alone)
      • A little bit of post-cog (watching the finger get chopped off -- the Immortal wasn’t happy the father didn’t listen. “Things used to be so much easier.”
      • A little bit of pre-cog (the father showing up alone)
      • Some other shit happens
      • the father receives another note, asking for more money and instructing him to go alone.
      • Rudy tells the father to go alone then return to the Inn when he’s done -- with or without his kids.
      • The father doesn’t show up by an agreed time, and Rudy heads to the place where he was instructed to meet the kidnapper.
      • Rudy finds the Immortal, with the father and the son, and still-bloody infant strapped to an elaborate draining-ramp, with a stopped reservoir at the bottom over a bath tub. The girlfriend is strapped to a table, her abdomen cut open with and the hastily severed umbilical cord hanging loose from the wound. Her eyes are glazed over. The Immortal is finishing his sentence, “... start with the youngest one, first.” (but make it more… occulty) as he cuts through the infant’s inner thigh, cutting the femoral artery, the blood gurshing out and down the ramp.
      • Rudy doesn’t hesitate -- he raises his bow and fires, piercing the Immortal’s temple and Frontal Lobe, but not killing him. The immortal falls to his knees and doesn’t move, though he is still breathing. He approaches, then slits the Immortal’s throat with a hunting knife.
      • Rudy checks the fiance and child -- both are dead -- and lets the two survivors down. They’re destroyed, obviously, and Rudy hands the tags and ring back to the son with a shy “I’m sorry… I was too late.” The son is visibly angry, punches Rudy. Rudy tells the father and son to stay there, and to call the police (the place needs to have a phone)... the father needs to insist that the body is incinerated, the sooner the better, and if there’s any trouble with that let Rudy know and he’ll take care of it.
      • He never calls.
      • A few weeks later, the Executive shows back up to Rudy’s to thank him, and asks if there’s anything he can do for Rudy -- Rudy suggests that the county could lose all the paperwork for the Inn, so he can focus on Hunting instead of worrying about code and taxes. The exec agrees, and that’s the end of that.
    • New case
      • A lawyer’s son is kidnapped -- he has no relation to the County Executive. Son is 12 years old, and has no pregnant fiance.
      • Lawyer received son’s finger in an envelope with a note with “Rudy - Shepherd Junction” written in it.
      • Lawyer shows up at the Inn, Rudy tries to shoo him away by saying he’s closed, and then when the lawyer says he’s not there for a drink but that he’s looking for Rudy, Rudy says he’s retired, but he can contact someone about it and have them there within an hour. Lawyer says, “I don’t think you understand… I’m not looking for just anyone -- I’m looking for Rudy at Shepherd Junction. I have something to show him,” as he pulls out the envelope.
      • Flashback of the Executive asking for the case, here.
      • Despite the differences, Rudy recognizes the similarity immediately. He takes the case. He asks questions, and then tells the father to keep his phone on incase he needs him. Somewhere in there is the line “First, my father slips into a coma, and now this…”
      • Thinking he knows how this is going to end, he loads up his truck and heads to the same place as the last case only to find it was bulldozed over and is now a vacant lot with a for-sale sign.
      • Rudy pulls out a beat up flip-phone and fishes a take out menu for a Chinese place out of his glove box, punching in the number. “Hey, it’s Rudy… I need to talk to the old man. I’ll be there in a half hour.”
      • Initial hunting flashback here.
      • Rudy heads straight to the back door and knocks -- it’s the middle of the night, all the businesses are closed nearby, but the door opens anyway. It’s a younger asian girl. “Right this way.”
      • The old man is older and wheelchair-bound, now, but it’s the same old guy (he’ll be dead before the next story). The ritual goes down in the same way -- Sees the finger chopped off, sees the father going alone, sees the child and an old man strapped to the old ramp.
      • Rudy thanks the old man in a hurry, paying him in cash for his services, and calls the lawyer, asking where his father is, and then tells the lawyer to meet him at the care facility.
      • Once there, the lawyer convinces the front desk that he needs to see his father -- he’s had a horrible feeling that he died, and he needs to see that he’s okay (delivered perfectly, fucking lawyers). Money may exchange hands. A nurse brings them back to the room he’s staying in.
      • The father looks at the grandfather, acknowledging that it is his father, and the equipment around him confirms he’s in a coma.
      • A nurse comes in while they’re there to check and rotate the patient. The lawyer assures them that there’s nothing he hasn’t seen so they’re fine to do it while they’re in there. He’s soiled himself, and they need to turn him anyway, so, with the help of a second nurse, they roll him up onto his side to clean him. While there, his gown falls forward, revealing everything, but most notably a perfectly normal looking left thigh.
      • The lawyer states that this is wrong -- his father has a massive scar, and is missing part of the muscle, on his thigh from a landmine in Vietnam. He was lucky and was only close enough to take shrapnel. One of the nurses agrees -- she’s seen the wound bathing him -- and, together, they all agree to check out the security footage. Rudy suggests looking at the time the grandfather slipped into a coma.
      • Back in the office, with the proper camera and time loaded up, they watch a skinny, middle-aged man in a suit drag a body from the front door, through the hallways, and into the grandfather’s room. Everyone ignored the pair as though it was totally normal. Shortly after, a nurse goes into the room, and after a few minutes comes out, crosses the hallway to a computer barely out of view, and begins typing. While she’s typing, the skinny man in the suit and the grandfather, now dressed in street clothes, walk out of the room and out of the building.
      • The nurse jumps into the EMR, and gasps when she pulls up the note that nurse was typing. “*Lawyer*, it says to have you call this number.”
      • The lawyer calls the number, while Rudy convinces the nurses he’ll get the grandfather back and they shouldn’t call the cops, and the person on the other end gives an address.
      • Rudy and the Lawyer head to the new location, and once there Rudy tells the lawyer to go in the front while Rudy works his way around the back. It takes longer for Rudy to get around than the Lawyer, so when Rudy makes it in, they’re already talking. “... didn’t expect you to come alone. Where’s Rudy?.. WHERE’S RUDY?!”
      • Rudy says something like “Right here…” and the new kidnapper turns to face him, leveling a gun at him. Rudy’s bow is drawn and leveled at the kidnapper. “I’m glad you’re here, you need to see this.” “I need to see what?” “You need to see what happens when you’re too late.”
      • That’s when Rudy recognizes the missing finger -- this is the Executive’s son, and he says so. “I’m sorry… I tried to make it...” “I’m sorry doesn’t cut it. ‘I tried’ doesn’t save *fiance* or my son.”
      • While the kidnapper is shouting at Rudy, the father works up the nerve to try and tackle the kidnapper. The kidnapper twitches and fires his gun at Rudy, hitting him between his first and second knuckles on his left hand. Rudy drops the bow, firing, but the arrow hits the ground and the carbon-fiber shaft splits and the arrow slides harmlessly across the floor.
      • The kidnapper quickly subdues the lawyer -- he’s military trained, and the lawyer is not. Just as the kidnapper dislocates the lawyers shoulder, Rudy is on him, and this fight is harder for both of them, with Rudy’s hand all fucked up. It quickly becomes clear that the kidnapper will kill Rudy if given the chance (fired bullets barely miss, etc), so Rudy changes his tactics from subduing to survival. Rudy can’t get the gun away from him, so he ends up wrestling to the ground, Rudy’s legs wrapped around the kidnapper’s, his broken and bloody left hand locking the kidnapper’s left arm behind him (hammerlock), and his right arm holding the gun arm with the gun aimed at the kidnapper’s head. “Drop the gun *name*... I don’t want to kill you, but I will if you don’t drop the fucking gun.” The kidnapper grunts, but instead of relenting actually dislocates his own arm to try and break free. Rudy forces his hand over the kidnappers, jamming his finger into the trigger guard, and the trigger pulls, shooting the kidnapper in the head.
      • (Stuff happens unrelated to the case)
      • Rudy delivers the dog tags and ring to the County Executive. He sighs, and looks close to tears. “I’m going to honor our agreement, and keep the Inn off the books, but I never want to see you again.” And takes off.

    Short story outline:

    • Three Acts Made Simple
    • Act 1
      • Inciting Incident: Lawyer comes with the case
      • 1st Turning Point: The original location has been bulldozed
      • How does this signal the end of the first act?
        • The easy way turns out to be a dead end, which means things are much more elaborate than they initially appeared.
      • How does this show that life will never be the same again for Rudy?
        • Rudy is retired, but he’s now neck deep in a case that’s obviously centered on him.
      • What is the dramatic question that needs to be raised?
        • How is Rudy going to catch the bad guy? What does this case have to do with Rudy?
    • Act 2 (Character development)
      • Point A: Flashback about the first case, look back on Rudy’s history and his reasoning for taking the initial case, and why he takes case in general (time in the military, history in Monster Squad)
      • Midpoint: The new vision
      • Point B: There is no child -- it’s the grandfather.
      • Second Turning Point: That’s not his father

    • Act 3

      • Stand Up: Rudy and the lawyer show up, lawyer goes in alone, Rudy comes around, new immortal monologues.
      • Climax: Rudy kills the new immortal
      • Ending: Rudy delivers the tags, Mickey exposes his grand plan, Mickey vows to kill Rudy.

    • Intro intro

    • Mickey drags the body from the car to the extended care facility. Missy and Harv follow, talking to each other, with Mickey occasionally telling them to shut up or someone will hear them. The auto-doors open for them, and the desk clerk says something like “damnit, the door sensor’s triggering off wind, again… better let maintenance know.”
    • Mickey struggles to get the body down the hall -- no one notices them, and one nurse goes as far as actively stepping over the body. As he passes the mail boxes, he notes a room number and keeps dragging the body toward it. When he gets there, the door is cracked but not open, so he waits a moment to make sure no one is there to see him, and he nudges it open then drags the body inside. Once inside, he lifts the body into a chair, propping it up in a way that he can see both the face of the man in the bed, and the face of the man in the chair. “Would it kill you to help me lift this guy? He’s heavy.”
    • Mickey licks his thumb -- it comes away bloody, not covered in saliva -- and begins to rub it into the man in the chair’s forehead. The man’s skin starts to become pliable, with a similar consistency to silly putty.
    • While he works, a nurse comes in to check on the man in the bed. Her eyes show surprise, and Mickey glances at her -- not even full attention -- and her eyes sag like she couldn’t care any less. He continues to rub blood into the man’s face, and looks at the nurse. “Stay here a moment and listen… I have a task for you.”

The teller is friendly, but the night manager is openly hostile toward Rudy, which ends in a threat from the manager to have the Inn shut down and a “Fuck you” from Rudy.

(within minutes, but enough for the lawyer to get his father and son down) Rudy tells the lawyer he’ll take care of the body.

Consider us even, and I never want to see you again.”
