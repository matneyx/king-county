Right now, your world is one where monsters are real, and the mystery is often *what* kind of monster or *why* it's doing something.

In **canon 2nd Edition**, killing a Huntsman is a **temporary victory**, not the end of the Hunt. ([White Wolf Wiki][1])

The reason is pretty awful.

When a Keeper creates a Huntsman, they **tear out its heart** and replace it with one of their own **Titles**. The Huntsman's real heart is hidden in the Bastion (dream) of a sleeping mortal, where the Huntsman itself can't retrieve it. ([White Wolf Wiki][1])

So if Jamie and Rudy simply kill the Huntsman:

* the Huntsman's body dies,
* but because its real heart still exists,
* it reforms somewhere in the Hedge (typically within about a month),
* and the Hunt begins again. ([Onyx Path Forums][2])

That's why the Wild Hunt is so terrifying in CtL: you can't simply kill your way out of it.

---

### There are three more permanent outcomes.

#### 1. Return the Huntsman's heart.

If the Huntsman is reunited with its original heart, the Keeper's Title is ejected and returns to the Keeper, while **that individual Huntsman is freed** from its enslavement. ([White Wolf Wiki][1])

This is probably the closest thing to a "happy ending" for the Huntsman itself.

---

#### 2. Destroy the heart.

If the heart is destroyed, **that Huntsman is gone forever**.

However...

The Keeper's Title simply flies back to the Keeper.

The Keeper can eventually implant that Title into another Huntsman.

So **the individual dies, but the office survives.** ([White Wolf Wiki][1])

That's an entirely different campaign.
