    Phoebe

    Alias/Nickname: Ashley (used in early brainstorming for What Wasn't and What Was — same character, treated as a naming inconsistency across sessions rather than a distinct person)

    Naming preference, confirmed (2026-07-16, decided live) — vault bookkeeping only, not a finished-manuscript decision: Now that Ashley exists as her own, unrelated named character in the Clara McKenzie material, use "Phoebe" throughout the vault's own prose and cross-references going forward, even when a source chat used "Ashley." The alias itself stays documented above as real naming history; only the loose vault-wide shorthand changes. Direct quotes that use "Ashley" (Dave's own words, drafted dialogue) are left verbatim rather than rewritten.

    What she's actually called in the published book: Sticking with "Phoebe" for now (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session) — not fully re-litigated, but no longer treated as urgently open either. The underlying tension is still on record: "Phoebe" risks being on-the-nose about the Monster Squad modeling (see The Monster Squad (Movie)), which is the same reason "Ashley" was floated as a rename in the first place — but "Ashley" collides with an unrelated character elsewhere in this vault. If a better final name surfaces later, it's still fair game to revisit.

    Role in Setting: Catalyst of the original supernatural events, Rudy’s primary motivation for staying in King County

    Based in: Asylum in King County, Colorado

    Background¶

    • Early Life and Connection to the Monster Squad: Phoebe was a young child during the events in California that inspired The Monster Squad (Movie). She was the youngest member of the group and had a naturally high affinity for the supernatural, showing early signs of Awakening that made her critical in closing the portal.

    • Role in the Portal Ritual, resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Phoebe sealed the portal herself via a spontaneous Awakening — Scary German Guy, an older mage, was genuinely present and involved, but not the source of the power. The earlier "used as his battery" framing is retired. Her raw magical potential at such a young age was the deciding factor, though the experience left deep, lasting trauma.

    • Confirmed family background (per "Astrology Birthday Guess," full data export): Awakened as a Mage in kindergarten — "way too damn early." Her parents divorced shortly after her Awakening. Had a brother who died when she was a teenager, under circumstances she still doesn't know. Confirmed Mage archetype: "more D&D Wild Mage than Technocracy" (Dave's own phrasing) — an uncontrolled/untrained caster, not an institutional or disciplined one.

    • Confirmed powers and asylum situation (per "Civilian Rifle Equivalents," full data export — Dave's own comp: "Wanda Maximov from Marvel meets Walter Bishop from Fringe"): Kept drugged in the asylum for two reasons — lucidity lets her warp reality, and she also just seems "crazy" to staff regardless. Critically, the drugs don't actually stop her mind: she's an unwitting master of Astral projection. Confirmed future plot beat: in a later book, she creates a pocket dimension for "Team Rudy" to hide from Mickey in. Confirmed real (2026-07-22, decided live): unknowing dream-contact with The Promethean, who's trapped in the Abyss alongside The Vampire — see the Lingering Psychic Connection note below for the mechanism.

    • An insider at the asylum, confirmed (2026-07-22, decided live): Someone on staff knows exactly what she is — a wild mage — and is the actual reason she's kept drugged. The institution itself isn't in on it; it just follows the care path already sitting in her chart, written by that person, without anyone else understanding why it's shaped the way it is. Still unnamed — see Development Notes & Unresolved Questions.

    • Physical limits vs. astral freedom, confirmed (2026-07-22, decided live): The drugs and her own catatonia genuinely stop her body — she's never overcome that. But they don't touch her mind. She's been astral projecting and wandering the Umbra's various layers freely, unnoticed by anyone at the institution, for who knows how long.

    • Astral projection's origin, confirmed (2026-07-22, per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation"): She learned it from being "locked in her mind" for so many years in her catatonic state — not an innate gift, a survival skill born of the trauma itself. It's also her established channel of communication with Angel; on the astral plane, Mickey and Angel are visually/perceptually distinct from each other, unlike in the flesh.

    • Magic-as-music metaphor, confirmed (same source, Dave's own idea): Phoebe explains her and Mickey's magic as two kinds of musician — some learn to play Bach note-for-note but can't adapt when something changes; some jazz musicians barely know what note they're playing but can play anything by ear. Mickey is the former, rigid and classically trained; Phoebe is the latter, improvisational. This is the thematic reason she can beat him where raw power alone wouldn't be enough.

    • Dream-contact with Rudy, confirmed concept, scene details deliberately open (2026-07-22, decided live): At some point, Phoebe reaches out to Rudy this way — almost certainly through a dream, using the same kind of logic as the Wheel of Time's World of Dreams (Tel'aran'rhiod): the dream space is both real and not, and things that happen there carry real weight. When and where this happens isn't pinned down yet.

    • Potential scene with Mickey on the Astral plane, floated (2026-07-22): Mickey is confirmed to eventually gain enough Auspex to Astral project and is already established as using it to harass/manipulate her one-directionally. A scene where the two of them actually meet and interact there is a real candidate, not locked. See Mickey Two-Dice.

    • Confirmed narrative function in What Wasn't and What Was (per the same chat): Never appears on-page — she's "a photograph. She's the kid Rudy couldn't protect... she's why he doesn't take kid cases and why he always does. She's a spectre and a memory, not someone who will see any screen time." Rudy visits her at the King County asylum, but less often than he feels he should — he'd "rather take a bullet than look Ashley in the eye knowing he can't help her." His move to King County was specifically to be near her, not incidental.

    • Post-Event Trauma: The intense supernatural exposure during the California events left Phoebe mentally scarred, unable to fully process what happened. This trauma has manifested as a largely catatonic state, punctuated by periods of awareness and occasional supernatural sensitivity.

    • Connection to The Promethean: During the Portal Incident, Phoebe formed an unexpected bond with The Vampire’s Promethean servant. Drawn to her innocence and latent magical potential, the Promethean saw in Phoebe a path toward understanding humanity. This bond may have anchored him to his Pilgrimage, inspiring his eventual turn against the Vampire.

    • Promethean’s Sacrifice: In a pivotal moment, The Promethean chose to sacrifice himself to save Phoebe and the Monster Squad, allowing himself to be drawn into the portal. This selfless act left a profound impact on Phoebe, who may retain a subconscious memory of his presence.

    Current Condition¶

    • State of Mind: Phoebe is mostly catatonic, with her consciousness appearing to withdraw in self-defense. This withdrawal may be psychological, a reaction to trauma, or a result of her early Awakening. She experiences brief periods of lucidity, though these are rare.

    • Connection to King County's Supernatural Energy, confirmed (2026-07-22, decided live): Not coincidence, and not really conscious destiny either — resonance. King County's fracture is the same ancient wound the Vampire briefly tore open at a different, more recent point in California (see King County, Colorado), and Phoebe's own Awakening pulled her into direct contact with that same wound when she sealed the breach. Something in her still carries that contact. A place already thin like King County is exactly the kind of place that can hold someone touched the way she was — not because anyone planned it, but because like calls to like. Her periods of lucidity correlating with local supernatural fluctuations are the clearest evidence of this; nobody around her, including whoever placed her at that specific asylum, actually understands why it "works" for her when nothing else has.

    • Rudy’s Visits: Rudy Williams frequently visits her in the asylum, even though she rarely responds. His dedication seems to provide her with some measure of comfort, as her occasional reactions are most pronounced when he’s nearby.

    • Lingering Psychic Connection, confirmed real (2026-07-22, decided live): Kinda, per Dave's own cosmology logic — the Astral plane and the Abyss are both layers of the Umbra (see King County Cosmology Framework: Astral = High Umbra, Abyss = Low Umbra). Phoebe's an unwitting master of Astral projection; the Promethean is trapped in the Abyss. Different layers of the same structure, plus a real trauma bond formed during the ritual, is enough to keep a real link open between them — not just waiting for his return, an active, ongoing thing.

    • Residual Effects of Disquiet: The Promethean’s Disquiet may have influenced Phoebe’s perception of the world, amplifying her sensitivity to supernatural energies. This could explain her occasional lucid moments, especially if the environment around her temporarily shifts or aligns with the Promethean’s Wasteland-taint.

    Notable Traits / Abilities¶

    • Potential for Awakening: Despite her catatonic state, Phoebe may still possess latent magical potential. Her early signs of Awakening hint that she could be capable of extraordinary feats if her consciousness were fully accessible.

    • Sensitivity to Supernatural Activity, confirmed: Phoebe senses changes in King County, Colorado's supernatural environment because it's the same wound she touched directly during the portal ritual (see above) — not a vague empathic gift, a specific resonance with that specific fracture. This could be an asset if tapped into, though her catatonic state makes it difficult to do so.

    Speculative "what if" — Monster Squad Sequel (What-If Idea)¶

    A hypothetical, explicitly non-canon sequel idea imagines an adult Phoebe who recovered enough to have a daughter of her own, called back into action when The Vampire returns. This directly conflicts with her established catatonic, institutionalized state above — treat it purely as a "what if," not a fact about her current condition.

    Meta/craft note — modeled on a real film: Phoebe is explicitly and openly modeled on the 1987 movie The Monster Squad, with "Ashley" (her established alias) chosen specifically after the actress who played the film's Phoebe. See The Monster Squad (Movie) for the in-universe propaganda-film convention and the confirmed callback-line device, including a candidate line for her: "He's not a monster."

    In What Wasn't and What Was (referenced as Ashley)¶

    • Rudy keeps a photo of her and a crayon drawing she made at age six — the drawing shows a monster being banished by a girl with light in her hands — locked in a drawer at Shepherd's Inn, alongside a Monster Squad photo.
    • Flashback fragment: her voice, "Don't let go, Rudy!", swallowed by a void. (An earlier draft pass of the same scene used a different line instead: "You believe me, right?" — an alternate/discarded phrasing, not necessarily replaced for good.)
    • Rudy recognizes the pressure of broken reality (e.g. the name-flicker at Caleb's vigil) as the same feeling from "the tunnels under town, before the portal opened — before Ashley screamed and the world tore open."
