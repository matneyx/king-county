    Out of Scope Ideas

    Running log of ideas surfaced in brainstorming chats that didn't get fully integrated elsewhere — discarded alternates, half-formed threads, or things that seemed tangential at the time. Kept here rather than dropped, since this vault is a brainstorming repo first and a source of truth second. Check this list against new chats to see if an idea resurfaces (a sign it's more load-bearing than it looked) or can finally be fleshed out.

    From "King County Chronicles 1 prewriting" (Google Docs dump, 2026-07-22) — name not adopted¶

    • Katelyn la Rouge — a Toreador vampire from Dave's separate Castle Dell, GA play-by-post game, Mickey's friend and possibly protégé there. Died in that game. Explicitly not being brought into these stories — flagged only because the name surfaced once with zero context, not because there's anything to build on.

    From "Jane Doe Investigation Plausibility" (full data export, 2025) — rejected alternate reveal¶

    • Caleb as a mannequin, Leanne as "a different kind of crazy" — a fully non-supernatural alternate version of the central reveal, floated and explicitly rejected by Dave in his own words: "More sad. WAY less fun." Kept the supernatural/belief-based reveal instead.

    From "Clue Evaluation Assistance" (full data export, 2025) — superseded pivot mechanism¶

    • Discarded early version of "what sends Rudy to the Hedge" — an earlier plan had clearing The Lawyer in the RV red herring directly reveal that the area is where Jamie originally came out of the Hedge, i.e. the RV-guy resolution itself was the pivot. This was superseded by the later, more developed "tall quiet man near the creek" bus-stop mechanism confirmed in "Jane Doe Investigation Plausibility" (see What Wasn't and What Was) — the RV-guy clearing and the Hedge pivot are now two separate beats, not one.

    From "Clue Evaluation Assistance" / "Jane Doe Investigation Plausibility" (full data export, 2025)¶

    • Christmas-themed final epilogue photo — explicitly rejected by Dave ("I kept going back to 'christmas' or some bullshit like that, but the only real option is...") in favor of the confirmed "First day of school" sign. See What Wasn't and What Was.
    • A parent saying something like "some shadow monster down by the creek" directly, in Act 1 — rejected by Dave as "too big a nudge."
    • A batch of "one more supernatural clue" options offered before the bus-stop/creek-riprap mechanism was developed: a flickering missing-poster date, a library checkout log signed "Caleb" in eerie handwriting, a photo that changes shirt color, a drawing of a boy holding hands with an antlered figure, Jamie saying "You were there. At the barn. Before." Dave's response ("I need to think this over. It still feels too coincidental") shelved all of them; superseded entirely by his own bus-stop/creek/"Detroit riprap" solution — see The Lawyer in the RV.
    • A fuller "tired, sympathetic cop who tried and failed" arc for Detective Marcus Cole — explicitly downgraded by Dave to "only a phone call" for this book.
    • Specific Hedge-proof mechanics for "Caleb was never there" (an inert/recycled Fetch, a failed True-Name ritual call, a blank trophy-wall slot) and a "the door was for Jamie, not Caleb" twist — both assistant-offered, neither confirmed by Dave. Only the beat itself (Jamie and Rudy get certainty Caleb was never in the Hedge) is confirmed.
    • A detailed "Good Enough" Hedge-closing ritual scene (mirror shard, iron railroad spikes, invoking Jamie's undisclosed True Name) — offered as a full draft, not confirmed; only the closing-the-scar concept itself ("like Loial closing the Waygate") is confirmed. See Jamie.
    • Assorted unconfirmed dawn/vigil filler lines — kid dialogue ("He comes when the light isn't awake yet," "I don't trust the sun until it's all the way up"), a parent line ("man with deer horns doesn't like when the birds sing"), and a batch of vigil-crowd filler (gas station smoothies joke, a dog howling to Garth Brooks, "Craig selling his trailer with California plates," Rhonda's peach pie) — all assistant-generated, offered at the end of the transcript with no visible user reply.

    From "California Incident Brainstorming" (full data export, 2025)¶

    • Patrick and his sister, and Eugene — cut characters — an early draft of The California Incident/The Monster Squad (group) included "Patrick" (with a subplot where teenage Rudy blackmails Patrick's sister — dropped by Dave as not working in today's fictional meta, making Rudy "a peeping tom instead of a consummate badass") and "Eugene" (who only existed to give the film's Mummy and Gil-Man analogues something to do — both of which were already written off as pure propaganda-coverup additions never actually present at the real incident). Neither made it into the final club roster of Rudy, Sean, Horace, and Phoebe.
    • San Bruno, CA / Silent Hill homage — the "Lore post" retelling of The California Incident was briefly set in San Bruno explicitly as a nod to the town that inspired the Silent Hill games, before being retconned again to Baton Rouge, LA. Neither replaced the vault's plain "small town in California" framing — see the open question on The California Incident.

    From "Pulpify your Story.txt" (2025-06-05)¶

    • Corrupt ex-hunter cop — a King County cop who used to hunt supernatural things, now compromised/corrupt. Doesn't match established Detective Marcus Cole. Also logged on What Wasn't and What Was's Backlog Ideas section.
    • Broken ex-exorcist pastor — used to run exorcisms, now drinks turpentine and hums nursery rhymes. Doesn't match established Reverend Tobias Gray. Also logged on What Wasn't and What Was.
    • Lie-detecting bar — a bar Rudy goes to where drinks won't stay down if you lie. Also logged on What Wasn't and What Was.
    • Haunted department store — a conspiracy of missing kids tied to a defunct department store with an unlit third floor. Also logged on What Wasn't and What Was.
    • Discarded pulp-style Huntsman — a more violent, visceral version (e.g. ripping out a secondary character's eyes) dropped in favor of the Gothic hat/shadow/mirror depiction. See The Huntsman.
    • Superseded early pulp clues — a bloody toy left behind after a child disappears; a knife stuck in Rudy's motel doorframe as a threat. Neither was filed anywhere; both were floated before the "shoe found still warm" clue and Gothic direction won out.

    From "Mystery Clues and Emotion.txt" (2025-06-05)¶

    • "Weird Americana" roadside stops — Jamie's postcards are written at kitschy Americana roadside stops, with Rudy having quiet reactions to witnessing it. Mentioned only in passing as something to brainstorm further; noted as an open thread on Jamie's page.
    • Three-clues-per-act mapping — intent to map the Caleb-reveal clues across the story's acts (roughly three per act, Chekhov's-gun style). No concrete per-act list has been generated yet. Noted as an open thread on What Wasn't and What Was.

    From "Escalating Tensions Rewrite.txt" (2025-06-04)¶

    • Discarded Hale-as-hunter/Fed concept — an early rewrite of a Rudy/Nathan Hale confrontation portrayed Hale as a hunter or Fed with a dark shared past with Rudy: a "Cairo" incident, a threat about "blood-bound" turning, having let a "compromised" ten-year-old girl burn, and Rudy once having left him to "rot in a basement" (implying Rudy once held or rescued him from captivity). The scene also called Rudy "a drunk in a flophouse," conflicting with his established sober, controlled characterization. Explicitly rejected as wrong for Hale, who has no supernatural connection at all (see Nathan Hale). The concept itself — a hunter-world rival with a buried, violent shared history with Rudy — might still be worth recycling for a different, as-yet-uncreated character.

Was., The Hollow Truth, If It Happened at All, Some Things Shouldn't Be Remembered, In the Place That Never Was. Also floated subtitle variants: "A Chronicle of King County's Lost and Found" and "A Record of Vanishing Things."
    • "Not perfect. Just good enough." variant riffs — alternates floated alongside Rudy's confirmed catchphrase, not adopted: "Not flawless. Just finished." / "Not sacred. Just scarred." / "Not pure. Just real." / "Not right. Just true enough." / "Not everything. Just enough."
    • Self-publishing platform research (Itch.io, Smashwords, Draft2Digital, Lulu, IngramSpark, Gumroad/Ko-fi) and rights/traditional-publishing logistics — pure business/logistics content, not story material, intentionally not filed elsewhere in this vault.

    From "Project Chronicle Summary.txt" (2025-06-02)¶

    • Offered, unconfirmed supernatural flavor for the grave scene (Act 2.8) — a breeze at the grave, a thorn sprouting from the soil, the word "Caleb" carved gently into the wood. None confirmed as adopted.
    • Offered, unconfirmed supernatural flavor for Leanne's revelation scene (Act 3.2) — a shift in wind, flickering lights, a Hedge flower blooming in the graveyard (a possible thematic tie between Jamie's Changeling lore and Caleb's grave). None confirmed as adopted.

    From "Mystery Plot Breakdown.txt" (2025-06-03)¶

    • "Feral believers" twist for the second missing kid — the second kid (confirmed addition, see What Wasn't and What Was) could be one of a local group of children with their own lore/beliefs, who went into the woods on purpose "to save Caleb." Offered, not confirmed.
    • "It wasn't him, it just sounded like him" — the second kid could reveal the Hollow used Caleb's memory as bait for Rudy specifically, not Caleb himself — reframing the whole confrontation as being about Rudy, not the missing kids. Offered, not confirmed.
    • Cryptic sequel-hook clue from the rescued kid — "Caleb told me to leave. He wasn't here, but I saw him in a dream. He said, 'Tell the man with the gray coat that I'm still waiting. And not to be afraid of the mud.'" Vague and unsettling by design; offered as a possible hook back to Rudy's past or a future book, not confirmed.

    From "Supernatural Horror Publishers.txt" (2025-06-02)¶

    • Publisher/imprint research (DAW, Baen, Tor/Forge, Angry Robot, Titan, Flame Tree Press, Crossroad Press, etc.) and fantasy word-count/Fiverr-proofreading logistics — pure business/publishing-practicalities content, not story material, intentionally not filed elsewhere in this vault.
    • Rural-noir-inspired scene ideas, critiqued by Dave as "weird for weird's sake" — raw material that needs the "a clue must lead somewhere" principle (see Noir and Hardboiled Style Guide) applied before any of it is usable, not confirmed canon:
    • "Where Blood Pools Under Ice" (Winter's Bone-inspired) — core theme: family loyalty vs. survival in a tight-lipped rural community. Leanne's community past the plow line won't talk to Rudy out of loyalty/code of silence, not fear. Staging: Rudy questions a local who's chopping wood — each time Rudy presses, the axe hits harder. Line: "There's no need to yell in King County. They've got other ways to draw blood."
    • "Where the Snow Doesn't Cover Everything" (Wind River-inspired) — core theme: grief, justice, and helplessness in a land that doesn't care if you live or die. A half-frozen body in a ditch that only floods during snowmelt; Caleb's jacket found nearby with no footprints in or out. Staging: Rudy stands at the edge of a frozen river, realizing a body dragged under wouldn't surface until spring — forcing a choice between risking hypothermia to search the ice or chasing a lead that might be false.
    • "Ghost Stories Your Grandpa Won't Tell" (The Bottoms-inspired) — core theme: kids uncovering evil the adults pretend doesn't exist. An old man in a nursing home (possibly Western Slope Nursing Care in Jensen), described as "too lucid to be ignored, too damaged to be believed," tells Rudy of a similar disappearance 30 years earlier in the same woods — at the time chalked up to "runaways and bad blood" — a dog howling all night, an empty crib the next morning. The name he gives checks out as someone "never born," but it appears twice on a drawing Caleb made. Worth revisiting deliberately: this would extend the grief-thoughtform mechanic into a recurring historical pattern in King County rather than a one-off, which could be a genuinely strong idea if given a real payoff per the clue principle above, rather than left as arbitrary flavor.
    • "The Place Where Things Fester" (True Detective-inspired) — core theme: old evil, buried in ritual, protected by silence and power. A symbol burned into a tree behind Leanne's trailer matches one carved into a desk in a long-closed one-room schoolhouse up a canyon — "the kind the sheriff's grandpa might've gone to. The kind no one mentions anymore." (No sheriff character is currently established in King County; this detail implies generational/institutional complicity if one is ever introduced.) Moss recognizes it: "It's not a warning. It's an invitation." Implies the pattern has repeated before, but "the town only ever loses the ones no one believes in."
    • Combo scene: the frozen body doesn't match Caleb's size but wears his jacket; in its pocket, a school photo of a girl who went missing in 1979, wearing the same jacket, case unsolved. The investigator from that 1979 case turns out to be the same man Caleb once said "wasn't real" — except he's alive, and afraid.
