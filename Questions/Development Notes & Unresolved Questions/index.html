    Development Notes & Unresolved Questions

    Rudy Williams¶

    • Mystery of the Vampire's Portal: Does the portal the vampire attempted to open in California have any lingering connection to King County, Colorado? Could it tie into the region's reputation as a supernatural no-man's-land? Resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Yes — King County's fracture is the same ancient wound the Vampire's ritual briefly tore open at a different, more recent point in California. Not caused by the 1986 incident; King County's weak point is much older. See King County, Colorado and The Vampire.
    • Fate of the Monster Squad Members: Are there clues in King County, Colorado about what really happened to the other members? Were their deaths connected, and if so, how? Partially resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Horace's fate is confirmed — mundane, adult death from pneumonia, unconnected to the case, deliberately honoring the actor who played him. Sean's fate is deliberately left open. Neither was actually hunted; both drifted from Rudy over his own growing obsession, not an external threat. See The Monster Squad (group).
    • Phoebe’s Condition: Is Phoebe's catatonic state purely psychological, or could it have supernatural causes? Does her early Awakening hint at a hidden power that could still be accessed?
    • Rudy’s Role in King County’s Supernatural Dynamics: How much does Rudy know about the supernatural “ecosystem” of King County, Colorado, and is he considered a respected hunter among others in the know? Could his presence be drawing attention, either from allies or enemies?
    • Unseen Forces: Who or what might still be hunting Rudy? Is there a specific supernatural force (a vampire faction, cult, or creature) tracking him?
    • Reputation Among the Supernatural: How much do supernatural beings know or care about Rudy’s presence? While he has a strong reputation among locals and hunters, supernatural creatures may have a different perception or even recognize him as a potential threat.
    • Secrets of Shepherd’s Inn: Are there any unexplored supernatural phenomena within Shepherd's Inn or the area surrounding it? This could potentially tie into the area’s general no-man’s-land status, hinting at lingering energy or a dormant power.
    • Network Expansion and Future Alliances: Could Rudy expand his network further, perhaps involving supernatural allies or factions who would benefit from his connections? His contacts are mostly human, but the county’s nature might open doors to surprising new alliances.

    Phoebe¶

    • Her actual published name: Sticking with "Phoebe" (reaffirmed 2026-07-22, grill-canon session — still not necessarily the final locked answer for the finished manuscript, just not worth re-litigating again right now). "Ashley" remains retired from working use (see Ashley naming collision).
    • Cause of Catatonia: Is Phoebe’s condition solely due to psychological trauma, or could a supernatural force be suppressing her consciousness? Could the ritual with the portal have left a lingering effect that holds her in this state?
    • Lucid Episodes: What triggers her rare moments of lucidity? Are they connected to King County, Colorado’s supernatural no-man’s-land status, and could they contain clues about her potential Awakening?
    • Possible Future Role: If she were to regain her full abilities, would Phoebe become a powerful ally, or might her traumatic experiences make her a threat to herself and others?
    • Connection to the No-Man's-Land: Is Phoebe's condition, or her presence in King County, Colorado, linked to the reason why supernatural creatures avoid the area? Resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Yes — resonance, not coincidence. Her Awakening put her in direct contact with the same ancient wound that runs under King County; something in her still carries that contact, which is why her lucidity correlates with local supernatural fluctuations. See Phoebe. Still open: Could she play a central role in resolving or revealing the area's mystery down the line?
    • Impact of the Promethean's Return: If The Promethean is still alive and returns, could this reunion with Phoebe trigger a release of her consciousness or aid in her recovery? Return mechanism/timing deliberately left open (2026-07-22) — revisit once that's decided.
    • Promethean's Influence on Phoebe's Awakening: Did The Promethean's presence accelerate or influence her magical Awakening? Still open — the bond itself is confirmed real (see below), but not this specific causal question.
    • Subconscious Memory and Manifestations: Does Phoebe retain subconscious memories of The Promethean? Her brief moments of lucidity might be connected to echoes of their bond, manifesting as vague feelings or glimpses of his protective presence. Resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Real, active psychic link, not just subconscious echo — Astral (High Umbra) and Abyss (Low Umbra) are adjacent layers of the Umbra, and the trauma bond from the ritual bridges the rest. See Phoebe and The Promethean.

    The Monster Squad (group)¶

    • True Purpose of the Portal: Was the vampire's goal simply to open a portal to the Underworld, or was there a specific entity or purpose for summoning this dark energy? Resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Pure Abyss-obsession, true Lasombra fashion — not power, not service to a greater entity, not ritual duty. He meant to drag the Abyss through into the town to engulf it, not to open a door and step through. See The Vampire. Still open: Does it have any connection to King County, Colorado's supernatural aura? — see the still-open item below.
    • Cause of the Squad Members' Deaths: Were the deaths of the other Squad members orchestrated by a supernatural force, or could other factions be involved? Partially resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Not orchestrated, at least for Horace (mundane pneumonia) and Sean (fate deliberately left open, but the estrangement from Rudy was personality conflict, not a threat). See The Monster Squad (group).
    • Scary German Guy's Whereabouts: What happened to him, and could he still be an ally? Deliberately left open (2026-07-22, grill-canon session) — not being pushed for an answer now.
    • Legacy and Influence: Are there other individuals or factions aware of the Monster Squad’s original activities? Could there be others who wish to either uphold or destroy the legacy of what the group did?

    United States Marine Corps¶

    • Classified Mission Incident: The mission that led to Rudy Williams's discharge remains a point of mystery. Could this incident be connected to supernatural forces, perhaps even tied to what he experienced as part of The Monster Squad (group)? Resolved (pre-existing, cross-referenced 2026-07-22, grill-canon session): Fully detailed on Rudy Williams's own page — Task Force VALKYRIE recruited him specifically because of his California-incident exposure. His unit was slaughtered by Doc Mabey's First Change; Rudy went AWOL hunting him for six months, had him in his sights, couldn't take the shot, and turned himself in. Dishonorably discharged. Doc is confirmed still alive/at large for a future book — that's the one genuinely open thread here, not a contradiction.
    • Ties to Military and Other Hunters: Rudy’s Marine background might draw the attention of other hunters or ex-military personnel in the supernatural world. Are there other former Marines or government operatives aware of the supernatural who could cross his path?
    • Military Training Adaptations: Has Rudy made specific adaptations to his Marine training to handle supernatural threats? This could include unconventional weapons, knowledge of supernatural vulnerabilities, or traps designed for creatures rather than humans.

    Scary German Guy¶

    • Current Whereabouts: Where did Scary German Guy go after the events in California? Is he still alive, and if so, does he remain connected to the supernatural world? Deliberately left open (2026-07-22, grill-canon session) — not being pushed for an answer now.
    • Knowledge of the Portal: He may know more about the vampire’s motivations and the nature of the portal, possibly even understanding why King County, Colorado is a no-man’s-land. If contacted, he could shed light on the area’s supernatural properties.
    • Interest in Phoebe: As the person who helped harness Phoebe’s magical potential, he may have a personal interest in her Awakening or a sense of responsibility for her current state. If he’s aware of her condition, he might be willing to assist Rudy Williams, but his approach would likely be pragmatic rather than sentimental.
    • Potential Links to Other Factions: Scary German Guy could have connections to groups like the Mysterium, a werewolf pack, or even Prometheans, given his knowledge of flesh golems. These ties could prove useful to Rudy if he learns how to leverage them.

    What Wasn't and What Was — Which Outline Is Primary?¶

    Five outlines now exist for this book: the original Act 1.1–3.6 outline (Rudy living in a Winnebago, sober and controlled, Leanne comes to the Inn, a fuller and warmer resolution); the Detective Noir alternate outline; the Character-Driven alternate outline; the Mystery Crime Thriller alternate outline; and the Save the Cat alternate outline. Which (or what blend) is the real plan for the book? Resolved (2026-07-15, decided live, grill-canon session): The original Act 1.1–3.6 outline is the primary, canonical plan for the book. The four alternates (Detective Noir, Character-Driven, Mystery Crime Thriller, Save the Cat) were a drafting exercise — running the story through the Novel Factory tool's genre-specific templates — not competing creative visions or parallel drafts. They're retired to non-canon status except for the pieces Dave separately and explicitly confirmed as real regardless of outline (the second-missing-kid addition from the Mystery Crime Thriller pass, now on the main What Wasn't and What Was page; the "belief/memory/truth, not violence, neutralizes the Huntsman" mechanic — see the cascading resolution below). Where an alternate's specific mechanism (motel room, kidnapping, a given ending) conflicts with the main outline, the main outline wins.

    Rye / Jamie¶

    • Rye's Fate: Did Rye survive the train accident, die from it, or come back changed (Hollowed or partway Lost, per Changeling: The Lost terms)? Deliberately left unanswered — the user declined to develop it further when offered. Resolved (2026-07-22, decided live, grill-canon session): Dead — killed protecting Moss from the Huntsman, which was disguised as the "railroad bull" that caused the train-yard accident. Deliberately confirmed to give the Huntsman a real, proven kill before the book starts, reinforcing it as a genuine threat and not just Rudy's red herring. See Rye and The Huntsman. Prologue Discrepancy (now three accounts): Jamie/Moss's Hedge-exit has been described three different ways across three chats: (1) a quiet, solo field/barn/truck escape; (2) a chaotic dawn arrival alone, with sirens and someone bleeding; (3) escaping the Hedge around age 12 with a group of fellow Changelings (the only survivor), then found bleeding on a King County back road and rescued by Rudy himself from a Huntsman. Same event told differently across sessions, different moments in Jamie's life, or genuine contradictions to reconcile — not resolved. Resolved (2026-07-15, decided live, grill-canon session): All three are the same event, and Dave filled in the full mechanism directly. Jamie was stolen as an infant/toddler, held in Arcadia for subjectively hundreds of years, aged only as far as needed for labor — other Changelings passing through eventually named the abuse for what it was, and it took decades more to find and commit to a Hedge opening. The escape was solo, not with a group. On exiting, disoriented by real sensation for the first time, Jamie hid in a barn (visible along with the trailer park from the exit point), was working up the nerve to run for the trailer park when the Huntsman blocked that path, and ran the opposite way instead. Rudy — leaving Tina's place at sunup while she still slept — didn't clock the danger until he caught the Huntsman's "man in a hat" form in his rearview mirror; he U-turned, threw open the passenger door, and shouted "Get in!," with the dust and sunrise backlighting revealing the Huntsman's true bear-and-antlers shadow. Confirmed same-day follow-up: Rudy rams the Huntsman with the truck — a gun was ruled out as too dangerous to trailer-park residents. This is both the book's Prologue and the origin of Jamie and Rudy's relationship. See Jamie.
    • Aftermath of Rudy Seeing Jamie's Mein: How does witnessing Jamie's true Mien in the Hedge reshape Rudy's perspective on his past cases? What might it cost him later? Raised but not developed. Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): It doesn't reshape his view of past cases. Rudy's never dealt with fae/Changelings in any meaningful way before, and he already knew Jamie was different long before seeing the Mien — the reveal confirms what he'd already accepted, rather than recontextualizing anything from his history. See Jamie.

    Rudy Williams — Who Is "She"? (resolved: Amber)¶

    A chat referenced Rudy updating his will so that an unnamed "she" — someone who "earns it" — would inherit Shepherd's Inn instead of Nathan Hale. The full "Escalating Tensions Rewrite" transcript surfaced a separate, fully drafted letter from Rudy to Hale (a brainstormed future/"Book 4" beat) that gives the Inn to Hale outright, with instructions to give Jenn half if he ever sells — strongly suggesting Jenn is "she." A second candidate, Amber, and a likely-redundant third (an unnamed protégé, per "Supernatural Horror Publishers Guide") were also in play. Not fully resolved. Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Amber — Dave confirmed directly ("I believe I meant Amber"). The mechanism: the Clara McKenzie case exposes her to the supernatural and proves to Rudy she's fully capable; combined with her existing bar experience, he hires her to cover shifts at Shepherd's Inn, which also solves the practical problem of not having to explain coming home covered in blood. The Jenn letter is superseded, rougher, earlier brainstorming, not an alternate answer. The "unnamed protégé" Book 7 candidate is just another reference to Amber, not a third option. See Amber.

    Rudy Williams — The Hollow Creek Rumor¶

    Hale tells Jenn an unconfirmed rumor that Rudy shot and killed a man near "Hollow Creek" years ago in the middle of the night — no body, no charges, just someone who stopped showing up and Rudy with blood on his boots. Never confirmed, denied, or explained anywhere in the source material — real crime, supernatural encounter Rudy can't/won't explain, or town gossip with no basis? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): True — the same event as "A Human He Killed": a young-generation vampire, killed proactively, whose body didn't decay on final death and so read as an ordinary human kill. See Rudy Williams.

    The Prodigal Son (Book 2) — New Open Questions¶

    • How did Rudy actually get Shepherd's Inn? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Rudy bought it himself with saved service back-pay and odd-job money; the Skinwalker case made it affordable (the previous owner was the Skinwalker's victim, family sold cheap to be rid of it). See Rudy Williams.
    • What is Mickey building, and why? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): The Three Generations Ritual construct (see The Three Generations Ritual). Allen and Frank are the nursing-home body-swap — Allen is fleshcrafted into a copy of the real target, Frank, so Frank can be abducted without staff noticing. See Mickey Two-Dice.
    • Is the Dawes case truly unresolved? Executive Dawes's kidnapped son's fate is never shown — only recalled as backstory once Jeff Speno's son goes missing under an identical MO. Are Speno's case and Dawes's case the same perpetrator returning, a copycat, or something else? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Dawes's case is The Three Generations Ritual's ~20-years-ago case — Dawes is the father, and his son survived (the girlfriend/fiancée and unborn child didn't). That surviving son, now grown, is "the kid" — Mickey's present-day manipulated assistant with a vendetta against Rudy (see Mickey Two-Dice). Speno's case is a new, unrelated family Mickey chose to recreate the ritual with — a recurrence of the pattern, not the same bloodline.
    • What is "Second King County Chronicle (Untitled)" now, if The Prodigal Son is confirmed Book 2? Resolved: Dave confirmed they're the same book — the generational-trauma/repeating-ritual themes are meant to be true of The Prodigal Son, with how they connect to the drafted chapters (the Dawes/Speno recurring MO, Mickey's warehouse ritual) to be worked out in future chats. See The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle).
    • Who is "the kid"? Mickey references an unnamed young assistant twice (can't get into the nursing home, has "his own shit to do," expected back before the warehouse project's next phase) — never named or described. Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session — identity confirmed same day): Executive Dawes's son — the now-grown survivor from The Three Generations Ritual's ~20-years-ago case, whose pregnant girlfriend/fiancée and unborn child died. Mickey is manipulating his grief, using it as fuel to help recreate the ritual and to go after Rudy — the kid genuinely wants revenge on Rudy for failing to save them; Mickey personally couldn't care less about Rudy at this point, he's just useful. Still unnamed on the page (only "Dawes's son"), but no longer a mystery whose son he is. This also resolves the previously-flagged "vendetta targets Mickey vs. Rudy" inconsistency on that page — it's Rudy.
    • What happened to Sarah? Jeff Speno's son's missing tutor — accomplice, second victim, or red herring entirely unrelated to the kidnapping? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): A Doll candidate — see Sarah.
    • LeGrande Station timeline conflict: Hale bought/renovated the station "three years" before this book's events, per the manuscript — in tension with the "roughly ten years" of feud established elsewhere. Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Six years — splitting the difference, since the exact figure doesn't matter much. See LeGrande Station.
    • The unnamed rival hunter group in Stanton City — mentioned once, never developed. Allies, competitors, or a future complication for Rudy's own network? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Likely not part of this continuity at all — Dave suspects this is cross-contamination from a separate, unrelated in-person King County tabletop game he runs for friends, not this book series. If they're ever used here anyway, they're allies, not competitors.
    • A smartphone appears in the Prologue (the nursing home guard), conflicting with the established "no smartphones exist in King County" tech rule from "Ghosts of King County." Slip to fix, or does the rule need revisiting? Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): No smartphones — a slip predating Dave's locked-down tech rules, fix in a future draft pass.
    • Do Allen and Frank resemble each other? The nurse who interrupts the Prologue scene addresses the sitting figure (Allen) as "Frank" before noticing Mickey — even though the real Frank is asleep in the bed. Could be a clue about why Mickey chose these two specific patients. Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session — supersedes an earlier same-day "parked as a manuscript slip" pass, before "Bridging Story Gaps" was reviewed): Intentional — Mickey is fleshcrafting Allen into a copy of Frank at that exact moment, and the nurse's mistake is the disguise visibly working. See Mickey Two-Dice.
    • Prologue prose vs. the "Harv can't physically interact/isn't perceptible to others" rule: Harv's dialogue ("if you let me... I offered... All you had to do was ask") and Mickey's line about Harv/Missy being overheard both read as though Harv had a choice and could be perceived by others — in tension with Dave's direct clarification. Resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session): Harv and Missy have full free will and autonomy — they can move around, observe, and eavesdrop independently, just without any meaningful physical effect on the world. Harv's "I offered... all you had to do was ask" almost certainly refers to offering to scout/watch/follow (things he can do), not to physically help drag a body (which he genuinely can't) — he's confirmed lazy about volunteering for that kind of task even though he's capable of it. Mickey's "they're gonna hear us" line remains an unresolved small wrinkle — no other entity has ever actually perceived Harv or Missy as of this book's events, so that line reads as Mickey's own confusion/paranoia about their nature rather than a real risk. See Harv.

    GUMSHOE Sources — Prepped But Never Used In Play¶

    A larger batch of Sources was brainstormed for Rudy's One-2-One game alongside the ones with existing pages (Detective Marcus Cole, Reverend Tobias Gray, Avery Greene, Nina Caldwell, Old Man West). These three have never come up in an actual session and have no pages yet — worth building out properly if/when they're actually used, rather than guessing at placement now: Professor Lydia Hawthorne (Anthropology/Archaeology/History/Theology, originally suggested for Montrose), Dr. Fiona Blackwood (Biology/Medicine/Pharmacy/Chemistry, originally Grand Junction), Professor Milton Larkin (Astronomy/Physics/Cthulhu Mythos, originally Vail). A fourth, Eliot Johnson (Medicine/Biology/Chemistry, a biochemist/former paramedic in Delta, deliberately differentiated from Aaron "Doc" Mabey), was iterated on twice but still hasn't appeared in a session.

    The Disappearance of Clara McKenzie — Open Threads from the Actual-Play Sessions¶

    Note (2026-07-22): This is a paused actual-play campaign — these threads are open because play hasn't continued, not because they're forks needing a desk decision. Leave them as session cliffhangers until the game resumes. - Is Detective Marcus Cole compromised? He spent the day actively helping Rudy investigate (IDing Zack Sawyer, pulling Danny's file), then his handwriting turned up on a warning note at Zack's ransacked trailer telling Rudy to back off. Genuinely dirty, impersonated/framed, or something else? Unresolved cliffhanger. - Who is Deputy Humboldt, really? New to King County (transferred from Nevada), yet knew Rudy's name on sight with no explained connection. Also the prime (unconfirmed) suspect in the missing cassette tape. (Name itself resolved 2026-07-16 as "Humboldt" — the one-off "Holcomb" was a misremembering, not a real alternate.) - What is Bram, really? Flinched when called out for knowing Rudy's name unprompted, then left town in an unexplained high-end Audi rather than continuing to hitchhike. The "famous musician in disguise" idea from planning was never actually confirmed in play. - Who sent Rudy the "We know you're in town" text? Arrived by blocked number right after Amina Kahn's visit — could be her unnamed employers, or something entirely unconnected to her. - Who is the woman at Zack's trailer? Resolved as Amber — see her page. - Who or what is The Broker? Zack's mysterious contact, offering him a way "out" of something for cash, the night he disappeared/was taken. Confirmed to hold an "Old Deal" debt over Detective Marcus Cole, but true identity/nature still unknown. - What happened to Clara, and what happened to Zack? Neither has been found, dead or alive. The "sacrifice... ritual in the woods" Clara overheard is still just a rumor at this point — never confirmed as real supernatural activity. A ruin at Raven's Creek is the next lead, not yet investigated. - Who is the "goth girl"? An unnamed young woman who led Clara, Zack, and Ashley deeper into the woods the night of the party, with a crew that hung back at the party's edge. Distinct from Ashley.
