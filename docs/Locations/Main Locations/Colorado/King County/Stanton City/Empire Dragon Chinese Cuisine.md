Run down Chinese food restaurant.

Also where [[Silas Freeman|The Fat Man]] does business.

**Detail confirmed in [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]], Chapter 3:** Closes before 10pm most nights, neon "Open" sign usually dark by the time anyone with real business shows up. The back alley shared with neighboring restaurants reeks of rancid deep-fryer grease and old leftovers — cordoned-off waste barrels, overflowing dumpsters, split garbage bags. Kitchen staffed by at least two young cooks (unnamed, one notably short-tempered) who speak Mandarin to each other and don't react much to strange requests. The dining room is dark hardwood, decorated with wall scrolls depicting the Chinese zodiac and warriors battling serpentine dragons, thick with smoke and fryer smell drifting from the kitchen. Silas holds court alone in a booth built for six, behind a bamboo-and-paper divider at the back — see [[Silas Freeman]] for his contact method (a fortune cookie with a custom, handwritten fortune).
