Old fur-trapper nickname for a remote clearing deep in the King County woods — "the largest clearing for miles in any direction," so dangerous "you'd better bring a boot-knife." Rumored territory for both Sasquatch and Wendigo sightings.

Fur trappers gave way to homesteaders, whose abandoned homesteads later became squats for teenagers hiding from local law (many of whom, ironically, had done the same thing themselves years earlier).

**Site of [[Executive Dawes]]'s ransom drop** for his kidnapped son, years before the present-day events of [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]] — the drop failed, and things escalated from there.

**Now redeveloped as Aspenview Estates** — a gated luxury housing development ("lots now available to build, 2+ acres"), six-foot stucco wall, wrought-iron keypad gate, private security patrol. [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] barely recognized the turnoff on his return years later; the old rocky, switchback-heavy back road was replaced with a freshly paved two-lane, and the mile markers no longer matched his memory. Went in armed with a flashlight, compound bow, and a full quiver of broadhead arrows (favoring quiet over a firearm) after scaling the wall to avoid the gate — the mostly-built lots, thick with young quaking aspens blocking sightlines between houses, took longer to search on foot than expected, and a slow-cruising private security patrol forced him to move carefully. Turned up nothing before he gave up around 3am and moved on to other leads. The irony of the transformation — cursed wilderness rebranded as yuppie real estate — is played for its own quiet dread: whatever made the place dangerous presumably didn't go anywhere just because the road got paved.
