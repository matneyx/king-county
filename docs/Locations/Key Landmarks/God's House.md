An old farmstead house standing alone in an open field — aged but loved, chipping paint, mismatched porch steps. Empty and dusty inside. Every wall is covered in handwritten prayer transcriptions ("Please help dad beat cancer," "please make sure Bosco finds a good home in heaven"). Opening any cupboard releases a whispered prayer, building to a cacophony if several are opened at once. Dave's own closing line for the concept: **"This is God's house, but God isn't home."**

**Confirmed (per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation"):** Reached by Rudy fleeing through a Hedge-adjacent dream/memory space, chased by buffalo the size of buses that knock down trees but won't enter the clearing around the house — they're driving him toward it, not hunting him. He's stuck there for a subjectively endless stretch (days, weeks), and nothing resolves until he writes a new prayer of his own — there's always paper but never a pen, so he ends up writing it in his own blood, drawn with a push-pin pulled from another note on the wall. He doesn't mail it (there's a mailbox, but that breaks the house's internal logic) — he pins it to the wall with the rest. The sequence doesn't end until he sleeps again after writing it.

**Confirmed later use:** Rudy was "fully gone" here once — genuinely lost/erased for a time, per his own history with the place. This is later why it works as a hiding spot from [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] near the series finale (see [[Book Order and Series Structure]]) — Mickey has no way to perceive into a place where Rudy was once erased.

**The bottle-episode this is built for, drafted (per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation"):** Dave's own pitch for a Silent-Hill-flavored haunted-house episode (placed around Book 6, after Jenn's left Rudy and Phoebe's beginning to reawaken) — Rudy in a snowstorm sees a little girl in the road, crashes his truck swerving, follows her figure into the wilds calling "Cheryl... is that Cheryl?" (it's actually Phoebe). Chased through a Hedge-adjacent dream space by buffalo the size of buses that knock down trees but won't enter this clearing — driving him toward God's House, not hunting him. Confirmed beat: at some point Rudy "nuts up" and marches toward the danger with a bow in hand and a cigarette in his mouth, saying **"I'm in this goddamn club, aren't I?"** — a deliberate *Monster Squad* callback, the moment it's clear who Rudy and Phoebe really are. He eventually wakes in the old Monster Squad treehouse. Dave explicitly rejected a "child-Rudy" framing for this sequence ("I don't really like child-Rudy that much"), but confirmed he wants the teeth motif and the Huntsman to resurface here, and liked the idea of figures from earlier books showing up as memory-echoes in the space generally. Floated, not confirmed: a *WandaVision*-style reveal that Phoebe herself caused this space, compared by Dave to "Sam Winchester scratching the wall between him and his soulless past."
