## From "Supernatural Horror Publishers.txt" (2025-06-02)
- **Publisher/imprint research** (DAW, Baen, Tor/Forge, Angry Robot, Titan, Flame Tree Press, Crossroad Press, etc.) and **fantasy word-count/Fiverr-proofreading logistics** — pure business/publishing-practicalities content, not story material, intentionally not filed elsewhere in this vault.
- **Rural-noir-inspired scene ideas, critiqued by Dave as "weird for weird's sake"** — raw material that needs the "a clue must lead somewhere" principle (see [[Noir and Hardboiled Style Guide]]) applied before any of it is usable, not confirmed canon:
  - *"Where Blood Pools Under Ice" (Winter's Bone-inspired)* — core theme: family loyalty vs. survival in a tight-lipped rural community. Leanne's community past the plow line won't talk to Rudy out of loyalty/code of silence, not fear. Staging: Rudy questions a local who's chopping wood — each time Rudy presses, the axe hits harder. Line: "There's no need to yell in King County. They've got other ways to draw blood."
  - *"Where the Snow Doesn't Cover Everything" (Wind River-inspired)* — core theme: grief, justice, and helplessness in a land that doesn't care if you live or die. A half-frozen body in a ditch that only floods during snowmelt; [[Caleb]]'s jacket found nearby with no footprints in or out. Staging: Rudy stands at the edge of a frozen river, realizing a body dragged under wouldn't surface until spring — forcing a choice between risking hypothermia to search the ice or chasing a lead that might be false.
  - *"Ghost Stories Your Grandpa Won't Tell" (The Bottoms-inspired)* — core theme: kids uncovering evil the adults pretend doesn't exist. An old man in a nursing home (possibly [[Western Slope Nursing Care]] in Jensen), described as "too lucid to be ignored, too damaged to be believed," tells Rudy of a similar disappearance 30 years earlier in the same woods — at the time chalked up to "runaways and bad blood" — a dog howling all night, an empty crib the next morning. The name he gives checks out as someone "never born," but it appears twice on a drawing Caleb made. **Worth revisiting deliberately**: this would extend the grief-thoughtform mechanic into a recurring historical pattern in King County rather than a one-off, which could be a genuinely strong idea if given a real payoff per the clue principle above, rather than left as arbitrary flavor.
  - *"The Place Where Things Fester" (True Detective-inspired)* — core theme: old evil, buried in ritual, protected by silence and power. A symbol burned into a tree behind Leanne's trailer matches one carved into a desk in a long-closed one-room schoolhouse up a canyon — "the kind the sheriff's grandpa might've gone to. The kind no one mentions anymore." (No sheriff character is currently established in King County; this detail implies generational/institutional complicity if one is ever introduced.) [[Jamie|Moss]] recognizes it: "It's not a warning. It's an invitation." Implies the pattern has repeated before, but "the town only ever loses the ones no one believes in."
  - *Combo scene*: the frozen body doesn't match Caleb's size but wears his jacket; in its pocket, a school photo of a girl who went missing in 1979, wearing the same jacket, case unsolved. The investigator from that 1979 case turns out to be the same man Caleb once said "wasn't real" — except he's alive, and afraid.
