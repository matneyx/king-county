A standalone origin story for how [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] and [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] first met — brainstormed but not resolved (the conversation ends mid-brainstorm on an open question). Set roughly 20 years before the "present day" of [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]/"Ghosts of King County," with period tech "roughly Y2K, 2005 at the most."

**This is very likely the concrete mechanic behind the "generational ritual" theme merged into [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]** (see that page's Thematic Layer section) — a repeating ritual across a family line, grandfather/father/child, with "at least two prior, unseen occurrences" implied. This case may be one of those prior occurrences. It also strongly echoes the Dawes/Speno kidnapping MO in The Prodigal Son (a finger mailed as proof/warning, a "no cops"/"come alone" demand, escalation after the family involves police) — worth treating these as the same recurring pattern rather than coincidence.

## The Ritual
A blood-mage's method for extending life: drain and bathe in the blood of **three firstborns across three generations of one bloodline** (e.g. grandfather, father, and child). The mage gains **three times** the combined remaining lifespan the victims would have had. Dave's own math example: a 60-year-old (10 years left), a 40-year-old (30 years left), and a 10-year-old (60 years left) sum to 100 years — tripled to 300 years gained.

**Likely connection to Mickey's current warehouse construct:** The three suspended autopsy tables draining via copper piping into a single bathtub (see [[Mickey Two-Dice]]) match this ritual's mechanics almost exactly — three victims, one collecting basin. Not yet confirmed as the same ritual on the page, but the parallel is hard to miss.

## The Original Case (~20 years before "now")
A father came to Rudy — his teenage son and the son's pregnant girlfriend had been kidnapped by the blood-mage. The mage's plan: use a money-for-victims ransom exchange as a ruse to kidnap the father too, then drain father, son, and the unborn child (the girlfriend's pregnancy providing the "child" generation) all at once.

- First contact: the mage mailed the father his son's severed finger, with a location and a "no cops" demand.
- The father initially involved police anyway; the ransom drop failed as a result.
- A second envelope arrived: a finger, and the note **"I said no cops."**
- Rudy was brought in only after this escalation — not from the start.
- **Outcome:** Rudy saved the father and the son. He did not save the girlfriend or the unborn child — they were killed. A partial victory with a permanent scar.
- The mage himself was killed by Rudy during this case.

## Mickey's Involvement (present day, unresolved)
Mickey seeks **Golconda** — or, failing that, immortality without risk of being consumed by his Beast. He somehow learned of this ritual and the failed attempt from 20 years ago — how exactly was an open "gap" when this specific conversation ended, with three options brainstormed and none chosen at the time:
1. Mickey found the dead mage's grimoire/notes (possibly cursed or warded), including a margin note with Rudy's name.
2. Mickey fed on someone connected to the case and absorbed impressions/memories of it.
3. A supernatural intermediary (a Seer, a Fae, an Ordo Dracul researcher) told him, for a price.

**This gap is very likely resolved in a separate, later chat ("Vampire Population Ratio," full data export):** Dave states directly, "The way I figured Mickey would find out about it would be some esoteric murderbilia — the original blood-bather's notes" — i.e. option 1 above. The two chats never cross-reference each other, so this is treated as a strong resolution rather than an assumption, but worth double-checking with Dave since the connection is inferred across sessions.

**The surviving son now has a vendetta against Rudy, resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session)** — not Mickey. He blames Rudy for failing to save his girlfriend and their unborn child. **He's also confirmed as "the kid"** — Mickey's unnamed present-day assistant in [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]] (see [[Mickey Two-Dice]]). Now grown, he's being manipulated by Mickey, who's using his grief as fuel — both to help recreate the ritual and to go after Rudy. The two have different motives in the same scheme: the kid genuinely wants revenge; Mickey personally doesn't care about Rudy at all at this point, the kid is simply useful to him. One still-unconfirmed idea for how their working relationship started: Mickey approaches the now-grown son looking for ritual info, the son refuses and attacks him, and that fight is how Rudy first hears of Mickey at all — no longer in conflict with anything, just not locked in.

**Likely connected scene — Rudy's epilogue line to the father (per "Vampire Population Ratio," full data export):** In the confirmed present-day case this ritual likely underlies (see [[Mickey Two-Dice]] for the reasoning connecting this to the Dawes/Speno kidnapping), Rudy tells the grandfather only a partial truth rather than the full story: *"He got mixed up in something and I couldn't save him."*
