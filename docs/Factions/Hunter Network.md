Rudy's loose network of hunters and investigators, covering a broad region from [[King County, Colorado|King County]] to Green River, Utah (see [[Rudy Williams]]).

**Sign system (unnamed):** Members and supernatural-aware travelers mark warnings and information using a variant of traditional hobo code — chalk or coal symbols left in the open, but with esoteric additions specific to the supernatural (not the TV show *Supernatural*, though it uses a similar concept of hunter-marked signs). Likely used to flag things like safe houses, active threats, thin spots, or "someone's watching this place."

**Occupational hazards of the profession (per [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]):** Alcoholism, depression, and lechery run rampant among hunters — suicide, overdoses, and untreated disease reportedly kill as many hunters as the actual job does. Rudy considers himself one of the lucky ones, being "well on his way to dying of old age," which he notes is practically unheard of in the profession. Reinforces why he set up shop in a bar specifically.

**A rival/unaffiliated group (per The Prodigal Son):** Rudy has heard a new group of hunters set up recently in [[Stanton City, Colorado|Stanton City]] — distinct from his own network, unnamed, and not yet interacted with on the page.
