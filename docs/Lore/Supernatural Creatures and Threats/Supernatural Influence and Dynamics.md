- **Quiet Presence**: Unlike more active supernatural hotbeds, [[King County, Colorado|King County]]’s ecosystem remains relatively subdued. Entities like [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] and [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]] seem to recognize the danger of drawing attention, and others like [[Aaron Mabey|Doc]] maintain a transient existence, keeping to the fringes.

- **Confirmed county-wide population breakdown (per "Vampire Population Ratio," full data export — Dave's own numbers, not just assistant estimates):**
  - **Humans:** ~200,000 total across the county (Stanton City-analog ~160,000, Montrose-analog ~20,000, Delta-analog ~15,000, Olathe-analog ~5,000).
  - **Vampires:** **7 total** — 5 pre-existing and unnamed, plus [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] and [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]].
  - **Werewolves:** Effectively **zero territorial pack presence** — the Western Slope is deliberately a *Werewolf: the Forsaken*-style "no-man's-land," inhospitable to werewolves for reasons still undecided, which is why [[Aaron Mabey|Doc]] being there at all makes him "a huge anomaly." This supersedes any earlier "1–3 werewolves" style estimate.
  - **Changelings:** [[Jamie|Moss]] confirmed. One additional drifter changeling was floated but explicitly hedged by Dave as maybe not part of the ecosystem — not firmly adopted, treat as an open possibility only.
  - **Mages:** [[Phoebe]] confirmed as a full Mage, but institutionalized and functionally removed from the power structure — the county has no active arcane oversight as a result.
  - **Hunters:** [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] is the one aware, active hunter in the county.
  - **Department of Truth:** Explicitly has **no meaningful presence within the county** (per Dave's direct instruction), though it still exists in the wider setting and may resurface later.
  - **Unconfirmed, brainstorm-only material not adopted:** an assistant-proposed King County vampire court structure (Prince/Sheriff/Harpy roles, domain-by-highway-corridor territories) was offered at length but never named, endorsed, or engaged with by Dave beyond the headcount above — treat all of it as raw option-menu material, not canon, unless Dave revisits it. Note: the "Madness Network" analog concept was actually raised by Dave himself, conditionally ("especially if King County has a Madness Network analog") — a hypothetical he floated, not something the assistant invented and Dave ignored, though it's still not a firm adoption either way.

- **Confirmed dramatic irony — Rudy believes King County has no vampires:** Dave's own framing: "I'm sure the local vampires are aware of Rudy, and do enough to stay off his radar. Every so often one doesn't succeed at that and Rudy offs them, and that's enough to keep him believing there are no vampires in King County." Mechanically, local vampires treat Rudy as a known hazard and avoid his notice; the rare vampire who doesn't (usually a nomadic drifter off I-70/rail) gets killed by Rudy, and each such kill *reinforces* his false belief rather than undermining it — he reads outliers as proof of absence. His hatred of vampires specifically (as opposed to general tolerance of other supernatural weirdness in the county) is rooted in [[The California Incident]], not general monster-hunting zeal. The confirmed payoff: King County actually harbors one of the most dangerous vampires Rudy will ever face ([[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]], post-diablerie of the returned Abyss-touched vampire — see [[Mickey Two-Dice]]), and the reveal is meant to land hard precisely because of how long Rudy's spent believing otherwise.
    
- **Unresolved Conflicts**: [[Aaron Mabey|Doc]]’s arrival and his past with [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] create potential for confrontation. His reason for seeking Rudy’s help is unknown, but their shared history and the trauma of their last encounter add tension. Similarly, Mickey’s presence in [[Stanton City, Colorado|Stanton City]] raises the possibility of conflict, as a Blood Hunt might attract unwanted attention.
    
- **Silas’s Prophecies**: [[Silas Freeman|Silas]]’s psychic abilities place him in a unique position of influence, allowing him to see glimpses of King County’s future. Though human, his powers make him a valuable resource for Rudy or others aware of the supernatural, provided they earn his trust.
