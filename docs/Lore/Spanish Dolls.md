A cross-country hunter-network nickname for a decades-long string of forensically-inconsistent look-alike bodies — [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]]'s fleshcraft/bonecraft attempts at sculpting other people into perfect copies of herself. Identical in gross appearance (scars, moles, general shape reproduced as closely as possible) but always forensically inconsistent on close inspection — different fingerprints, dental records, mismatched implant serial numbers.

**The name itself:** Dave's own deliberate detail, flagged explicitly as an in-world ethnic-stereotype naming convention among hunters, not an authorial slip — Kayla is Brazilian, and the hunter network calling the pattern "Spanish" anyway is meant to read as characteristic hunter-culture carelessness: "brown is brown, Mexican is Spanish, and so stereotypes go into naming things."

**Why the pattern stayed hidden so long:** Wide geographic and temporal dispersion — [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] and Kayla have historically kept moving, so no two Dolls have ever turned up close together in place or time.

**Origin location:** The earliest known Doll cases were near a fictional city called **Castle Dell, Georgia** — the same Castle Dell where Mickey's human self, Michael Turino, was later committed to an asylum (see [[Mickey Two-Dice]]). Likely not a coincidence.

**Why Dolls don't become "passengers" like Missy, Harv, and Kayla:** The identity-erasure and torture the Dolls undergo leaves them psychologically shattered ("Gnawed") by the time they're embraced — no coherent self left for a soul to anchor to. When Mickey diablerizes a Doll, the soul dissolves completely, unlike victims (Missy, Harv, Kayla) who retained enough identity to persist inside him.

**Book 1 seeding, confirmed as the established [[Gil]] (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session):** [[Gil]] mentions the Dolls to [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] in passing at Shepherd's Inn, as one of many odd hunter-network rumors — Rudy dismisses it without following up. This is the same Gil already established as a Shepherd's Inn regular — his "armchair Hunter" characterization (knows the lore and the talk, has never done the work himself, but stays plugged into that world) fits perfectly with passing along a rumor like this one.

**Confirmed future plot beat (not yet placed in a specific book):** Dave wants two Dolls eventually created and killed by Mickey in the same county — one diablerized, one killed mundanely — with the significant detail being proximity: the first time two Dolls turn up clustered together, possibly the first time in the same state. That clustering would signal to the wider hunter network that "the Dollmaker settled down," putting King County on outside hunters' radar for the first time.
