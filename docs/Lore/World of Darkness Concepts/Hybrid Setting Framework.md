King County deliberately blends World of Darkness (WoD, classic lines like Vampire: The Masquerade, Werewolf: The Apocalypse, Mage: The Ascension) with Chronicles of Darkness (CoD, the modernized lines: Requiem, Forsaken, Awakening) rather than picking one. This explains why the vault already mixes terminology from both across [[Lore/World of Darkness Concepts]] (e.g. Malkavian Clan and Blood Hunt from classic Vampire, alongside Awakening and Mysterium from Mage: The Awakening).

**Base approach:** Use CoD as the mechanical backbone (streamlined rules, Conditions/Beats/Integrity) while plugging in classic WoD lore and cosmology as needed.

**Fusion pillars (brainstormed correspondences, not all confirmed as canon):**
- **God(s):** The God-Machine (CoD) as a shell/mechanism left behind or hijacked by greater entities like the Wyrm or Weaver (WoD).
- **Underworld:** Layered — CoD's bureaucratic Underworld (Geist) on top, WoD's Oblivion/Tempests below, with old Spectres seeping upward.
- **Spirit Worlds:** Hisil (CoD) as a localized spirit reflection, with the Umbra (WoD) as a broader metaphysical cosmos beyond it, accessible via deeper Hisil zones.
- **Vampires:** Requiem as the default (no Caine, local myths), with Masquerade's Cainite mythos (Caine, Jyhad, Gehenna) reduced to just one powerful Blood God's cult rather than universal truth.
- **Mages:** Awakening's Watchtowers/Lie/Exarchs as default, with the Exarchs framed as what became of the Ascension-era Technocracy, and the Watchtowers as rewritten Spheres.
- **Werewolves:** Forsaken as default ("the ones who stayed behind"), with Apocalypse-style Garou framed as returning after a banishment (or vice versa).
- **Ghosts/Spirits/Demons:** Wraith: The Oblivion as "deep" ghosts, Geist: Sin-Eaters for surface-level present-day ghostly concerns; Demon: The Fallen and Demon: The Descent as two aspects of the same metaphysical rebellion (the Fallen are old Descenters who stayed behind).

**Candidate in-world justifications for why both cosmologies coexist (none confirmed as chosen for King County specifically):**
1. **The Shatter** — a recent cosmological event, or the slow failure of the God-Machine, lets deep Umbra leak into the Hisil; the Gauntlet weakens, old gods return, Cainean vampires wake, and the Ascension War was only the beginning.
2. **The Lie is Fraying** — Mage: Awakening's "Lie" starts collapsing, and old-WoD truths (Caine, Antediluvians, Apocalypse prophecies) seep into the vacuum; the Exarchs and the Wyrm turn out not to be so different.
3. **Multiple Realities** — the Chronicles world is a rebuilt copy of the original WoD world (the God-Machine's reconstruction after a "Time of Judgment"), and something got loose from the old version.

**Character creation:** Use CoD mechanics, but let players pick Origins and Belief Systems from either line.

**Supernatural template blending, additional specifics:**
- **Vampires:** Requiem Clans/Covenants as default, with Masquerade's Camarilla/Sabbat included as rival political orders — possibly remnants from a previous age. Most vampires follow local origin stories rather than the Cainite myth.
- **Werewolves:** Forsaken mechanics as base, with Apocalypse tribes and spiritual enemies folded in as legacy spirits or "old blood" — e.g. the Silent Striders reimagined as exiled Forsaken with deeper umbral ties.
- **Mages:** Mechanically Awakening, with the Ascension War as setting history and the Technocracy reframed as an ancient, failed attempt to control the Lie.

**Implementation notes:** Start local (a single region where the bleed-through is strongest — presumably [[King County, Colorado|King County]] itself, given its established "no-man's-land" reputation) and build localized myths for why a given faction is there (e.g. "the Camarilla exists here because of something specific to the area"); write with mystery rather than over-explaining; draw themes from both lines (WoD: epic tragedy, cyclical destruction, cosmic horror; CoD: intimate horror, surveillance, entropy, moral decay); consider cross-line factions (a Banisher/Wyrm-corrupted pack, a Kindred cult of the Abyss tied to old Tremere, a Sin-Eater who hears Oblivion's voice).
