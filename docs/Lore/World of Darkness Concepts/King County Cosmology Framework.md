Confirmed by Dave (per "Civilian Rifle Equivalents," full data export) as the loose, deliberately-unofficial mapping this setting uses to unify concepts across different World of Darkness game lines, since there's no White Wolf license: "the same, but different," loosely unified rather than mechanically precise.

**Confirmed mappings:**
- **Astral plane = High Umbra** (Werewolf: the Apocalypse/Forsaken terms borrowed for a Mage-flavored "high" spirit realm).
- **The Abyss and the Underworld = Low Umbra.**
- **Arcadia is its own separate thing**, but also functions as *the* dream realm in this setting's cosmology — not folded into the Umbra structure at all.

**Confirmed tone guardrail (Dave's own words, worth preserving verbatim rather than paraphrasing):** The wound/scar/tear underneath [[King County, Colorado|King County]] is cosmic in scale, but the series is explicitly **not** meant to become Cosmic Horror in tone as a result — a deliberate genre guardrail, not an oversight. Compare [[Werewolves and Spirit Worlds|the discarded "Rusting Place"/Book 3-5 cosmic-horror scaffolding]], which pushed harder toward that register in speculative brainstorming that was ultimately not adopted for the earlier books.

**Confirmed county-specific application:** [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] is aware of the Astral plane (and is confirmed to eventually gain enough Auspex to project into it, using this against [[Phoebe|Ashley]]) despite having zero awareness of the Hedge — the two realms are treated as genuinely separate tracks of supernatural knowledge, not a single unified "the supernatural" a character either knows about or doesn't.
