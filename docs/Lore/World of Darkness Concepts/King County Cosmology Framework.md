Confirmed by Dave (per "Civilian Rifle Equivalents," full data export) as the loose, deliberately-unofficial mapping this setting uses to unify concepts across different World of Darkness game lines, since there's no White Wolf license: "the same, but different," loosely unified rather than mechanically precise.

**Confirmed mappings:**
- **Astral plane = High Umbra** (Werewolf: the Apocalypse/Forsaken terms borrowed for a Mage-flavored "high" spirit realm).
- **The Abyss and the Underworld = Low Umbra.**
- **Arcadia is its own separate thing**, but also functions as *the* dream realm in this setting's cosmology — not folded into the Umbra structure at all.

**Confirmed tone guardrail (Dave's own words, worth preserving verbatim rather than paraphrasing):** The wound/scar/tear underneath [[King County, Colorado|King County]] is cosmic in scale, but the series is explicitly **not** meant to become Cosmic Horror in tone as a result for the earlier books — a deliberate genre guardrail, not an oversight. **Correction (2026-07-16, fresh-eyes pass on the full "Werewolves and Spirit Worlds" export):** this guardrail applies to the early books, not the whole series — Dave separately and explicitly confirmed Book 5 itself *is* cosmic horror ("Book 5 is definitely cosmic horror," see [[Book Order and Series Structure]]) in the same chat. What actually got discarded from that chat was the Book 3 Fetch/"Rusting Place"-rescue plot and Rudy's memory-immunity trait (see [[Aaron Mabey]]) — not the Book 5 cosmic-horror escalation itself. The previous version of this note conflated the two and linked to a nonexistent "[[Werewolves and Spirit Worlds]]" page; fixed to point at where that material actually lives.

**Confirmed county-specific application:** [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] is aware of the Astral plane (and is confirmed to eventually gain enough Auspex to project into it, using this against [[Phoebe]]) despite having zero awareness of the Hedge — the two realms are treated as genuinely separate tracks of supernatural knowledge, not a single unified "the supernatural" a character either knows about or doesn't.

**Standing rule — mechanics are bookkeeping, not lore (reaffirmed 2026-07-22, decided live):** Dave tracks game-mechanical detail (Discipline names and dot ratings for [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]], Contract/power roles for Moss in [[The Huntsman]], etc.) purely to keep character capability internally honest and consistent — none of it is ever meant to surface on the page by name. The books read as a Hunter/noir story on the surface; the underlying game logic stays entirely invisible to the reader.
