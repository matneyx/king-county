**Alias/Nickname:** None known

**Physical:** 5'9" was the figure used early in "Ghosts of King County" during initial character/portrait description, but 5'7" is what actually got used once actual play started — confirmed explicitly in a later scene in that same chat ("Rudy is only 5'7"") and echoed again in the separate "Escalating Tensions Rewrite" chat (notably shorter than [[Nathan Hale]], a former state champion wrestler). Treat 5'7" as the settled figure; 5'9" appears to be an early draft number Dave moved off of once play began. Wiry, skinny-but-muscled build. Rockabilly style: snap-button cowboy shirts, heavy black work boots, leather jacket, chin-length brown hair slicked back greaser-style, blue eyes, a wallet chain (per [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]). Chain smoker despite knowing better — specifically Camel Wides, per the same source. Visual references cited: a mix of James Intveld, Johnny Depp, and Skeet Ulrich, with tired eyes like Steve Buscemi.

**Role in Setting:** Veteran Monster Hunter, Protector of Phoebe, Survivor of supernatural events

**Based in:** [[Shepherd's Inn]] in [[Shepherd Junction, Colorado]]
#### **Background (Pre-King County)**

- **Origins**: Rudy was part of a group of kids who survived a supernatural encounter in [[California]], inspired by the true events behind [[The Monster Squad (Movie)]]. He was one of the only people who understood the real stakes of the situation, not the fictionalized version. As a teenager, he helped prevent a powerful vampire (not Dracula, as in the movie) from opening a portal to a realm—potentially Limbo, the Abyss, or the Underworld. **Confirmed dates:** [[The California Incident]] happened in 1986, when Rudy was 15 and [[Phoebe]] was 5.

- **Confirmed dates (military and arrival):** Enlisted in the Marines just before the Gulf War (Operation Desert Storm) and deployed to Kuwait, ~1990–91. Left the Marines around 1999 at roughly age 28, with zero interest in reenlisting after 9/11. The solo game's "present day" is set roughly 15 years later, around 2014, making Rudy about 43.

- **Supernatural Battle**: During the [[California]] events, a local werewolf pack tried to take down the vampire but failed, with one member ending up blood-bound to the vampire. Rudy and his friends enlisted the help of a mage (known as “[[Scary German Guy]]”) to close the portal. [[Phoebe]], showing signs of [[Awakening]] at a young age, acted as a battery for [[Scary German Guy]]’s ritual to close the portal.

- **Aftermath**: The experience left deep marks on all the survivors. Rudy served time in juvenile detention and then joined the [[United States Marine Corps]], while the others went separate ways. [[Phoebe]] suffered severe mental trauma and has spent her life primarily in mental health facilities.

* **A civilian framing of the juvenile-detention-to-Marines pipeline (draft dialogue, consistent with established canon):** In his own words to Jenn, before any of the supernatural stuff: "It wasn't monsters the first time. Wasn't justice. Just me. Angry. Broke. Broke a lot of things. They gave me a choice — prison, or the war." He chose the war "because it was honest, at least." Frames his current hunter activity as breaking the same kind of laws now, "just tr[ying] to do some good with it."

* **Fallujah anecdote (separate from the Kuwait supernatural-ops unit):** Claims to have been "a private champ in a bar outside Fallujah" — took down a man twice his size without spilling his beer. Implies a second, non-supernatural military deployment/story distinct from the Desert Storm-era Kuwait unit where Doc Mabey turned.

* **A third deployment detail (per The Prodigal Son):** a 16-month deployment in Saudi Arabia, described as "a good soldier" but worn down by "four years without the ability to make real, life-changing decisions." Possibly compatible with the Kuwait and Fallujah deployments as part of one longer enlistment rather than a contradiction — not confirmed either way.

* **History with Doc Mabey**: Rudy was part of an elite special forces unit in Kuwait where they were tasked with dealing with supernatural threats during Desert Storm.  During one mission, [[Aaron Mabey]] experienced his First Change and slaughtered everyone in the unit except Rudy.  Rudy went AWOL hunting Mabey, unsuccessfully, for about six months before he was discharged and sent home.

#### **Arrival in King County and Establishment (1999–Early 2000s)**

- **Transition After the Military**: Rudy arrived in [[King County, Colorado|King County]] in 1999 after being discharged from an elite military unit specializing in supernatural threats. With no initial knowledge of the county’s supernatural undercurrent, he began his new life living out of his truck-bed cab-over camper—a rugged 1972 Ford F250 with an extended bed, which he still drives. He worked odd jobs, quickly building a reputation as a reliable handyman around King County and surrounding areas.

- **First Encounter with Local Supernatural Threats**: When local cattle began mysteriously disappearing, Rudy’s past experiences with supernatural entities drove him to investigate. He initially suspected a werewolf but discovered it was a [[Skinwalkers|Skinwalker]]. Though not well-versed in the nuances of local shapeshifters and Lycanthropy, he successfully handled the threat, marking his unofficial initiation as a “fixer” for the county’s hidden supernatural issues.

- **Shepherd’s Inn**: Grateful for his help, the locals pooled enough money to allow Rudy to purchase [[Shepherd's Inn]], a roadhouse in [[Shepherd Junction, Colorado|Shepherd Junction]]. He used it as his home base and a public front, while locals with unusual problems—some supernatural, some not—began coming to him for solutions. Over time, even the county police quietly recognized his expertise, occasionally recommending him when cases involved something “too weird” for standard protocol.

- **Conflicting origin story (per [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]], Book 2):** This manuscript tells it differently — Rudy drives west after a 16-month deployment in Saudi Arabia specifically because he'd heard his last surviving Monster Squad friend, "Ash" (almost certainly [[Phoebe]], whose established alias is Ashley), had been relocated to a facility in the Rockies. He lives out of his truck camper for about a month, taking odd jobs (hunting and mundane work both), before finding the abandoned Shepherd's Inn near a railroad siding and buying it outright with money saved during his enlistment — "the bank... was practically giving it away." No mention of the Skinwalker case or the community pooling money to buy it for him. The two origin stories aren't reconciled — worth deciding deliberately which is canon, or whether the Skinwalker case simply happened later, after he already owned the place. See [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]].
- **Shepherd's Inn's legal immunity (per The Prodigal Son):** The Inn is entirely exempt from county code enforcement — never inspected, never will be — as a personal favor from a county official grateful to Rudy for saving his son's life. Almost certainly [[Executive Dawes]], whose kidnapped-son case (see below) is the likely source of that debt, though it's not stated explicitly on the page.
- **The Dawes case (Book 2 flashback — Rudy's first King County case):** Not long after buying the Inn, county official Executive [[Executive Dawes|Dawes]] came to him — his son and the son's fiancée had been kidnapped, ransomed at a clearing called [[Boot Knife]]. Rudy's identity/reputation as a hunter isn't explained on the page; Dawes simply says he "hear[d] you... hunt things." The ransom drop failed (police involvement, against Dawes's instructions, may have spooked whoever was meant to collect it), and a second envelope arrived demanding a solo retry — accompanied by a severed adult finger. The outcome isn't shown; it's recalled only as backstory once an identical MO resurfaces with [[Jeff Speno]]'s son in the present day, strongly implying the case was never truly closed.
#### **Growth of a Network and New Role (Mid 2000s–Present)**

- **Building a [[Hunter Network]]**: As Rudy’s reputation spread, he began cultivating a network of like-minded problem solvers, drawing on contacts from Colorado’s Western Slope and parts of Utah. Recognizing that supernatural cases are sparse in any single county, he established a loose network of hunters and investigators who could respond to issues as needed, covering a broad region from King County to Green River, Utah.

- **Home Base at Shepherd’s Inn**: Rudy now lives in a Winnebago parked behind [[Shepherd's Inn]], with access to the Inn’s kitchen, bathroom, and shower. His old camper still stands beside the RV, but he no longer uses it for living. He maintains a low profile, generally leaving only for critical issues within [[Shepherd Junction, Colorado|Shepherd Junction]], while primarily acting as “dispatch” for the hunters in his network. Deliberately not a permanent trailer home — it's something he can hitch up and leave with if necessary.
-
- **Role as Dispatch and “Superior”**: Rudy coordinates jobs, hears rumors, and provides guidance to hunter cells, passing on tips when something unusual pops up. He serves as a mentor and “superior” for clients who request one, establishing himself as the go-to resource for anyone in need of help with paranormal issues. In many ways, he has become a legend in the local supernatural circles, a central hub for dealing with King County’s otherworldly dangers.

#### **Current Role in King County**

- **Relocation to King County**: Rudy moved to [[King County, Colorado]], Colorado, after his discharge from the [[United States Marine Corps]]. He came specifically to be near [[Phoebe]], who is catatonic and confined to an asylum in King County. His sole mission is to protect her from the unknown forces he believes are still after them.

- **Personal Mission**: Rudy suspects that the deaths and disappearances of the other [[The Monster Squad (group)]] members are directly related to the supernatural events they experienced. This suspicion keeps him vigilant and isolated, always anticipating that whatever targeted them before might come for him or [[Phoebe]] next.

* **Relationship with Local Law Enforcement:**  [[King County, Colorado|King County]] law enforcement discreetly acknowledges Rudy’s expertise, occasionally recommending him off the books for cases that seem beyond standard protocol. 

#### **Current Reputation and Roleplaying Hints**

- **Local Legend**: Among locals and those “in the know,” Rudy is the guy to contact for supernatural issues. Known for his reliability, discretion, and no-nonsense approach, he’s trusted by hunters, police, and wary townsfolk alike. He’s respected but feared, especially as he rarely reveals specifics, keeping details about supernatural threats close to his chest.
- **Combat Style and Approach**: While Rudy doesn’t often go out in the field anymore, when he does, he’s known for his aggressive style—jumping into fights without weighing the odds. He’ll throw himself into a brawl if necessary, especially if something disrupts Shepherd Junction. For most cases, though, he prefers to act as the mentor, guide, and dispatcher, making sure others can do the work without his direct involvement.

#### **Key Relationships**

- **[[Jenn-with-two-Ns|Jenn]]**: His grounding force in [[What Wasn't and What Was]]. Where Rudy reads every hardship as a supernatural sign or consequence, Jenn refuses to mythologize pain — she's lived through real hardship with no curse behind any of it, and her non-magical read on events is played as correct, not naive. **Confirmed: Jenn does not know about the supernatural, and Rudy intends to keep it that way** — she sees his humanity, not his war; she cares for him because he's kind when he doesn't have to be, not because she knows what he's faced.
  - **Caretaking behaviors (established via draft dialogue):** Brings her peanut brittle and licorice when she's had a bad shift; once pulled her car out of a ditch at 2am and never told a soul; calls her "kid" — established as armor, not an insult.
  - **Jealousy/protectiveness tell:** Reacts badly to [[Nathan Hale]] saying her name "like he owned it" — pushes back even though Hale doesn't mean it that way.
  - **The Hollow Creek rumor (unconfirmed, hanging plot thread):** Hale tells Jenn a specific rumor about Rudy: that he shot and killed a man near "Hollow Creek" in the middle of the night — no body, no charges, just someone who stopped showing up and Rudy with blood on his boots and no explanation. Neither confirmed nor denied anywhere in the source material. Jenn's response is to trust Rudy anyway ("Rudy's never lied to me... it makes him real," "I'll take dangerous and honest over clean and fake any day"). Whether this rumor is true, exaggerated, or refers to something supernatural Rudy can't explain is unresolved — see [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]].
  - **The "bear" framing (brainstormed thematic dialogue, not confirmed as adopted lines):** Riffing on the "would you rather be alone with a strange man or a bear" meme, the idea floated is that Jenn would choose Rudy over the bear specifically because his danger is honest and known, unlike most men's — candidate lines include "I've seen him angry — and I've seen him choose not to be" and "Neither is a bear. But at least a bear doesn't pretend to be my friend first."
  - **Reciprocal protectiveness:** Per a brainstormed future (`"Book 4"`) beat, Rudy's protective instinct extends even to people he can't stand — including Hale — because "someone has to" stand between danger and people. See the Book 4 note on [[Book Order and Series Structure]].

- **[[Phoebe]]**: Once a bright and energetic child, Phoebe is now catatonic, likely due to the trauma from the events in [[California]]. Rudy feels a deep responsibility for her, viewing his presence in [[King County, Colorado]] as a way to protect her.
    
- **[[Scary German Guy]]**: The mage who helped them during the [[California]] events. His role in Rudy’s life was brief but pivotal. If he’s alive, Rudy might still consider him an ally, though his whereabouts are currently unknown.
    
- **[[The Monster Squad (group)]] Survivors**: The other members are all presumed dead or missing, and Rudy suspects foul play tied to their shared past. Their fate haunts him, pushing him to stay vigilant against any threat targeting him or Phoebe.
    
- **[[Detective Marcus Cole]]**: Rudy's contact at [[Brindle, Colorado|Brindle]] PD in [[What Wasn't and What Was]] — Cole gets him access to reports, footage, and patrol logs off the books when Rudy needs eyes on something before it gets buried in paperwork.

- **[[Jamie]]**: Per one (unreconciled) account of their history, Rudy found young Jamie bleeding and terrified on a King County back road, running from a Huntsman, and saved them — possibly killing or banishing the Huntsman — which cemented Jamie's loyalty to him. Repeated attempts to place Jamie in foster care failed; Jamie kept running back to him. Jamie eventually disappeared into the wider world (hopping trains, living off-grid) but returns occasionally to Shepherd Junction. Rudy knows about and hates Jamie's drug use (used to suppress memories of Arcadia and force sleep) but understands it. See [[Jamie]] for the continuity note on conflicting accounts of how they met. Already loves Jamie and knows they're different well before he ever sees their true Mien in the Hedge — when he does, he doesn't reach for a weapon or flinch; his first reaction is practical ("Am I gonna come out looking like that?"), and the shock is less about the monstrousness than about how much of Jamie stays the same underneath it.

**Keepsakes:** Keeps a drawer at [[Shepherd's Inn]] with a Monster Squad photo and a crayon drawing [[Phoebe|Phoebe/Ashley]] made at age six (a monster being banished by a girl with light in her hands). A flashback fragment tied to this: her voice, "Don't let go, Rudy!", swallowed by a void.

**What spare money is for:** Rudy doesn't hoard the cash he doesn't spend on himself — he keeps it ready for bailing someone out, or burying someone who doesn't have family left.

**Gothic sensory detail (offered, not confirmed adopted):** Rudy bleeds a little every time he reads a certain case file — just enough to stain the page.

**"Good enough":** Rudy's habit of rigging broken things at the Inn with duct tape and twine rather than properly fixing them (an early draft example: a broken railing), muttering "Good enough" — a recurring line/motif in [[What Wasn't and What Was]] that echoes later when Leanne must decide whether love and belief are "good enough" to hold Caleb in existence. Full phrase, confirmed while discussing author catchphrases: "Not perfect. Just good enough."

**Phone manner:** Answers the Shepherd's Inn landline with a clipped "Shepherd's."

**Cigarettes as avoidance:** Buying cigarettes at [[LeGrande Station]], despite finding the place an eyesore, is established as routine avoidance behavior rather than just habit.

**A blessing/curse:** Absolute strangers tend to be too open and forward with him — people blurt things out, ask invasive questions, overshare. Established via a gas station clerk pressing him about Jamie's gender (see [[Jamie]]).

**Weapons/equipment (per [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]):** Carries a Colt 1911 strapped at the small of his back, under his belt. For a stealthier stakeout/search (scaling the wall into [[Boot Knife|Aspenview Estates]]), he opted for a flashlight, compound bow, and a full quiver of broadhead arrows instead of a firearm.

**Per the [[Detective Noir Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Detective Noir alternate outline]] (not reconciled with the above):** An old injury has left a plate in one leg, aching and recurring throughout — broken again in the climax. Keeps iron bullets and a cold iron knife "from his war days." Possibly grew up near/in a trailer park himself, which is part of why Leanne's situation feels personal to him. This outline also characterizes him as drinking too much and talking to people who aren't there — in real tension with his established sober, controlled characterization (a similar "drunk" framing was explicitly rejected once before for a different scene; see [[Out of Scope Ideas]]). Treat this characterization with caution rather than as settled.

**Per the [[Save the Cat Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Save the Cat alternate outline]]:** Implies Rudy swore off using his old magic/hunter methods at some point before the story starts, and going into the Hedge means breaking that vow (undeveloped — what exactly he swore off, and why, isn't specified). In this outline's escalation, Rudy himself starts to forget Caleb's face as the Huntsman closes in — in tension with the established "Rudy is the only one who still says Caleb's name" beat on the main outline (see [[What Wasn't and What Was]]). In the "Stage 12: The Fallout" epilogue, he keeps a crayon drawing inscribed "To Mom" taped to his window (yet another version of the recurring ambiguous "drawing" object — see [[Caleb]]) and isn't fully sure he's really left the Hedge, but holds onto the fact that he remembers Caleb as proof he won.

**Rudy's will and a mysterious successor (now substantially clarified by the full "Escalating Tensions Rewrite" transcript):** Rudy keeps a will that isn't fixed — he updates it at 3am over cheap coffee, crosses names out with motor oil still on his hands, mutters "asshole doesn't deserve it," and keeps it in a drawer under his cleanest gun. In one framing (from the "Supernatural Horror Publishers" chat), [[Nathan Hale]]'s name is in it by default/technicality, and Rudy wants to cross it out "with the thickest black line" if an unnamed "she" shows up and "earns it" — implied to inherit [[Shepherd's Inn]] instead of Hale.

**Confirmed candidate for "she": [[Amber]]**, from the "Ghosts of King County" actual-play campaign — now confirmed by Dave as **Book 3** in the same numbered series as [[What Wasn't and What Was]] (Book 1), meaning this shares one continuity rather than being a separate parallel use of the setting. That puts Amber in direct tension with the earlier [[Jenn-with-two-Ns|Jenn]] theory (from a brainstormed "Book 4" letter) — both can't simply be true in unconnected stories anymore. Likeliest reading: the Jenn letter was earlier, less-developed brainstorming that Amber's confirmed Book 3 role has since superseded, but Dave should confirm deliberately rather than this being assumed. See [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]] and [[Book Order and Series Structure]].

#### **Notable Traits / Skills**

- **Military Training**: After serving time in juvenile detention, Rudy joined the [[United States Marine Corps]], where he gained significant combat and survival skills. He was discharged as the sole survivor of a dangerous mission, deepening his survivor’s guilt.
    
- **Survivor’s Guilt**: Having survived multiple traumatic events (both supernatural and military), Rudy carries a heavy sense of responsibility. His experiences have hardened him, making him resourceful but also distrustful and isolated.
