Pursuing [[Jamie]], who fled through [[The Hedge]] from [[Arcadia]] (Changeling: The Lost concepts).

**Gothic depiction (current direction for [[What Wasn't and What Was]]):** Never shown in full — only glimpsed as a hat, a shadow, a fingertip brushing the edge of a mirror. Dread over spectacle.

**Discarded pulp depiction:** A more violent, visceral hunter — willing to mutilate a secondary character (e.g. ripping out their eyes) just to provoke a reaction from [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]]. Dropped when the book's tone shifted toward Gothic horror.

**Origin:** Comes from the crook-path (part of [[The Hedge]]), a tangle of time-rotted hedge-thorns wrapped in glinting iron — jaw unhinged from years of quiet service.

**Confirmed nature (per the [[Detective Noir Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Detective Noir alternate outline]]):** Not just a creature but a function — "a servant of doubt, a predator of belief." Shape-shifts between a man, a beast, Leanne's face, and Rudy's own face. This directly parallels the "villain is disbelief itself" theme already established for the Caleb plot — the Huntsman may be a literalization of that same force, rather than a separate threat. Vulnerable to iron and sage. Its taunt: "You don't believe in yourself. You never did. That's why they die."

**Note on conflict:** This full-contact, shape-shifting depiction is shown fully in a climactic fight in the alternate outline, which is in tension with the Gothic depiction above ("never shown in full"). Not reconciled — may reflect two different drafts of the same climax, or the Huntsman being shown more directly only at the very end of the book.

**Resolution (per the alternate outline):** Not destroyed — "you can't destroy doubt" — but has its power cut away and is made irrelevant. The mechanism: Rudy secretly plants copies of Caleb's missing flyer all over town beforehand, regrowing the town's collective belief in Caleb, which makes the Huntsman vulnerable (a direct use of the collective-belief mechanic — see [[What Wasn't and What Was]]).

**Consistent with a second alternate outline:** The [[Character-Driven Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Character-Driven alternate outline]] arrives at a similar (not identical) resolution independently — the Huntsman "dissolves, not killed, but starved," its story rewritten when Rudy speaks loving, specific memory of Caleb into being rather than fighting. Two different drafts agreeing that belief/memory (not violence) is what neutralizes it is a signal this mechanic is likely to stick regardless of which outline wins.

**A third alternate outline, a third resolution mechanic, and a fifth appearance:** The [[Mystery Crime Thriller Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Mystery Crime Thriller alternate outline]] describes it yet again differently — "a towering, faceless entity with antlers like dead trees and a cloak made of rotting leaves and stolen names." New stakes rule from this draft: killing it outright would be a mistake, because "if you kill it, the Hollow eats what it has" (i.e. whatever/whoever it's holding). Speaks in echoes of Rudy's own doubts rather than its own voice. Resolved the same way as the other two alternates: Rudy defeats it by naming truth (Caleb's reality) rather than fighting.
