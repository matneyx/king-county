[[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]]'s alternate identity — surfaces when Mickey's Malkavian Memory Lapses derangement erases the very concept of "being Mickey."

**Origin:** Tied to a real person from Mickey's human life as Michael Turino — a servant Turino murdered out of jealousy back in Argentina. Everything Mickey isn't: kind, empathetic, genuinely well-liked by [[Missy Chamberlain|Missy]], [[Harv]], and [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]].

**Asymmetric awareness:** Angel knows about Mickey. Mickey has no idea Angel exists. Not an absolute rule, though — Dave's own hedge: "They don't share memories, except when they do."

**How an episode ends:** The fleshcraft disguise heals away on its own during ordinary vampire daysleep/torpor, since it was self-inflicted — Mickey simply wakes up "Mickey all over again," with no deliberate reversal needed.

**Self-aware martyr, not a survivor:** Angel has fully accepted that killing Mickey will erase him too. He isn't trying to survive — only to end the cycle for good. He and [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]] share an established, almost intimate ritual of re-crafting his face together via fleshcraft.

**Confirmed role in the Kayla arc:** After a Blood Hunt was called on Mickey, Kayla helped fleshcraft him into Angel specifically to escape it. She later conspires with Angel to destroy Mickey for good, accepting that this destroys Angel too. Mickey remembers the betrayal afterward and kills her for it.

**First on-page appearance (inferred to connect to [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]'s kidnapping case — see [[Mickey Two-Dice]] for the reasoning):** [[Missy Chamberlain|Missy]] points [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] out to Angel as "the man that stopped it," in a scene deliberately left unexplained on the page. Since only Mickey and Angel can perceive Missy, and fleshcraft has already been shown on-page by this point, the intended reader reaction is a "huh?" moment — registering something's subtly wrong between Missy and Angel without yet understanding what.

**Personality and habits, confirmed (2026-07-22, per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation"):** Bisexual (Mickey/Michael is straight). Wears Prada — Mickey wears cheap. Smokes cigarillos, prefers actual cigars if available (Mickey smokes cheap cigarettes). Has the **Eat Food** Merit — can genuinely eat and drink, including wine — which Mickey does not have; when Mickey wears Angel as a disguise, he still can't eat or drink and will reject/vomit anything he tries, which functions as an in-story tell for which persona is actually "awake." The reverse never happens — Angel never wears Mickey's face; if it's Angel, he always looks like Angel. Less linguistically gifted than Mickey — picking up Portuguese from [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]] (who's teaching, not fluent herself), he keeps slipping into Spanish by habit.

**How he finds Dolls, confirmed (same source):** He and Kayla run a bisexual-swinger-couple routine together as their way of selecting and luring victims — Angel knows Kayla creates the Dolls and knows what he himself is, he's just not cruel about it the way Mickey is.

**Drafted prologue scene, not locked as final prose (2026-07-22, per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation" — for the hypothetical Angel/Mickey-focused book):** Opens with Angel and [[Kayla Fawston|Kayla]] speaking partly in Portuguese — Kayla's teaching, not fluent herself, and Angel keeps slipping into Spanish since he's less linguistically gifted than Mickey. [[Missy Chamberlain|Missy]] and [[Harv]] have a side-conversation about being rude, assuming Angel/Kayla are talking about Missy; Harv attempts an imperfect translation, knowing less than Angel and no Spanish at all. Kayla, noticing the ghosts and dismissive of them: *"Oh, they're still here? I kinda figured they'd leave with Mickey."* Dave's own note: "Angel and Kayla have serious Spanish soap opera vibes."

**Book 2/3/4 recurring-cameo structure, confirmed (same source, specific placements pinned down 2026-07-22 via "King County Chronicles Book Map.xlsx"):** Appears at the end of Book 2 (the Missy/Rudy pointing-out scene above). Buys cigarillos from Jenn in **Book 3**. The swinger-act Doll-hunting sighting with Kayla happens in **Book 4**. Operates as "Angel" full-time through Books 3 and 4 doing reconnaissance — the shift back to being "Mickey" full-time happens around the start of the unnumbered Mickey/Angel side book (his own main-POV book, slotted right after Book 4).

**Draft death scene, not locked as final prose (2026-07-22, per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation"):** Angel in front of a mirror, saying goodbye to Kayla even though she's already gone. Pours an unfinished drink. Writes one line: *"You were right, Missy. He's not me. I hope I was."* Then burns. The body twitches, heals, and Mickey rises in his place: *"Fucking melodramatic, Angel."* Wipes the ash off, straightens his collar, gets back to work. This early draft has Mickey surviving the fire outright — later material (see [[Mickey Two-Dice]]) instead has Angel's actual sacrifice happen at the series finale, walking into the sun after Mickey loses Kayla and Phoebe both; treat this mirror-scene version as an earlier pass at the same beat, not a separate, additional death.

**His actual finale role, confirmed (same source — see [[Mickey Two-Dice]] and [[Book Order and Series Structure]] for the full sequence):** After Mickey discovers Angel and Kayla's betrayal and kills Kayla for it, and after Mickey loses Phoebe (pulled to safety by Moss) at the height of the final battle, the resulting grief is what finally brings Angel forward at full strength. Angel ends the cycle the only way he believes he can — by walking into the sun, killing Mickey along with himself. He goes in with no illusions about surviving; ending Mickey matters more to him than continuing to exist.
