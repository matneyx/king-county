**Aaron “Doc” Mabey**

**Type**: Werewolf (Tribeless)

**Background**: Doc was part of [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]]’s elite military team and underwent his First Change in the field, slaughtering the rest of the unit. Rudy went AWOL to hunt him for six months before the military discharged him. Doc has recently come to [[King County, Colorado|King County]], seeking Rudy’s help with an undisclosed matter.

**Confirmed escalation (per "Astrology Birthday Guess," full data export):** Rudy's command considered Doc a low-priority "rogue asset" and was ready to reassign Rudy to a new unit without chasing him down at all — the trigger for Rudy's AWOL wasn't just grief or vengeance, but the conviction that if Doc had to be put down, it should be by someone who still remembered him as a friend, not a stranger executing a kill order. Rudy took government firearms with him when he went AWOL, which is what actually escalated his discharge to dishonorable (and possibly a military prison stint) rather than a simple administrative separation.

**Speculative Book 3+ material (per "Werewolves and Spirit Worlds," full data export — explicitly NOT locked in, see [[Book Order and Series Structure]]):** A once-considered, later-discarded storyline had Doc trapped in a trauma-born decaying otherworld nicknamed "The Rusting Place," rescued by Rudy in a book that predates Dave's later direct confirmation that Book 3 is the Clara McKenzie campaign. A "Fetch" version of Doc living a normal life in King County, and a "Rudy is immune to memory-erasure" trait, were both explicitly rejected by Dave and should be treated as abandoned, not canon.

**Confirmed werewolf-specific mechanics and backstory (per "Civilian Rifle Equivalents," full data export — a major, separate worldbuilding session):**
- **Ahroun** (Full Moon/warrior auspice), despite being the unit's medic — Dave's own reaction: "that tracks with the Full Moon." **Homid** — born fully human, unaware of his nature until his First Change.
- Was genuinely skilled at fieldcraft, camping, and hunting before enlisting — real wilderness competence, not just soldier training.
- Explicitly lacks the social skills to survive in a city post-Change (doesn't know how to get a fake ID, for instance), so he's avoided civilization entirely ever since, sticking to wilderness and remote biomes.
- **Confirmed post-Kuwait travel route:** East out of Kuwait, across into Alaska via Russia (possibly by fishing boat), down through Alaska toward his home region (Idaho/Montana), then south into Colorado to find Rudy. Crossed paths with other hunters along the way who never clocked what he actually was.
- Tribe floated as **Children of Gaia** by the assistant — never explicitly confirmed or rejected by Dave; treat as plausible, not settled.
- After his court-martial and discharge, Rudy tells himself Doc (or whatever Doc's become) is dead — a deliberate coping mechanism, not a factual belief he's ever tested.
- **Major confirmed future plot beat:** one of the books — specifically the one that examines *why* King County is a werewolf no-man's-land, without packs policing the local spirit world — opens with **Doc showing up at [[Shepherd's Inn]], asking for Rudy's help.** Dave's own framing ties this directly to the setting's "no coincidences" rule: it's not lost on Doc that Rudy just happens to be guarding the exact area where whatever Doc is now tracking is located.

**Current Activity**: Doc haunts the rural areas between [[Jackson Ridge, Colorado|Jackson Ridge]], [[Jensen, Colorado|Jensen]], [[Red Bridge, Colorado|Red Bridge]], and [[Brindle, Colorado|Brindle]], steering clear of populated areas and avoiding trouble. His presence has not attracted hunters, and he has maintained a low profile, likely wary of drawing Rudy’s attention.

**Confirmed county-wide anomaly (per "Vampire Population Ratio," full data export):** King County's Western Slope is deliberately a *Werewolf: the Forsaken*-style "no-man's-land" with effectively zero territorial werewolf presence, for reasons still undecided by Dave. Doc being there at all makes him "a huge anomaly" rather than one werewolf among several — see [[Supernatural Influence and Dynamics]] for the county's full confirmed population breakdown.
