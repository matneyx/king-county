**Location**: [[Brindle, Colorado]]

**Abilities**: Forensics, Bureaucracy, Psychology

**Background**: A seasoned detective with a knack for solving cold cases. He has a deep understanding of forensic science and how to navigate police bureaucracy.

**Setting**: Based at the Brindle Police Department, he would have a modest office adorned with case files and photographs of past investigations. He might also frequent the local diner to chat with informants.

**Relationship with [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]]:** His contact at Brindle PD in [[What Wasn't and What Was]] — gets Rudy access to reports, footage, and patrol logs off the books when Rudy needs eyes on something before it's buried in paperwork. Rudy's line when asking: "Not pulling rank. Just need eyes on it before someone buries it in paperwork."

**Personal life:** Has a teenage daughter, Lena, going through a "rebellious phase." Restoring an old Indian motorcycle in his spare time — the carburetor has been giving him trouble for months. Rudy and Marcus are old, easy friends; their calls open with genuine catching-up, not just business.

**A serious unresolved twist (Disappearance of Clara McKenzie case, 2024-11-07):** At [[Zack Sawyer]]'s ransacked trailer, Rudy found a handwritten warning note he immediately recognized as Marcus's handwriting: "This should've ended years ago, Rudy. You're not supposed to know any of this. Leave it alone, or someone else will end up in the ground." No signature — just the handwriting. This directly conflicts with Marcus's portrayal earlier the same day as a helpful, trustworthy ally actively helping Rudy investigate Clara's disappearance (he pulled Danny's file, identified Zack Sawyer by his tattoo, and sent Rudy Zack's address). Whether Marcus is genuinely compromised, being impersonated/framed, or something else entirely is completely unresolved — a major open question. See [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]].

**Confirmed (per GM notes generated during play): Marcus is indebted to [[The Broker]]** through an "Old Deal" from the past, serious enough to have pushed him to act against his own moral compass — this is likely the actual mechanism behind the warning note, though it hasn't played out in a scene yet. Still actively working the Clara/Zack case with Rudy afterward (radioed [[Deputy Humboldt]] away from a confrontation with Rudy at a Brindle church), suggesting he isn't in "deep enough" to have skipped town or cut contact — whatever the debt is, he's still trying to help Rudy within its limits.
