Young King County Sheriff's deputy, recently transferred from Nevada.

**Name inconsistency in the source material:** called "Deputy Holcomb" once, then "Deputy Humboldt" for the rest of the session — not reconciled, pick one.

Responded to [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]]'s deer collision on the way to [[Stanton City, Colorado|Stanton City]]. Knew Rudy's name on sight despite Rudy not recognizing him and Humboldt never having been to [[Shepherd's Inn]] — unexplained, and it unsettled Rudy enough to note his name for later. [[Detective Marcus Cole|Marcus Cole]] confirmed Humboldt is new to the department and shouldn't have any obvious reason to know Rudy.

Mentioned recent strange noises in the woods (more than the usual coyotes) and a handful of unproven local disappearances, advising people to stick to main roads after dark.

**Suspected (unconfirmed) of taking the cassette tape** from Rudy's truck during the traffic stop — the tape vanished sometime during the encounter, and no other explanation has surfaced.

## Follow-up confrontation (played scene, church behind the Brindle service station)
Turned up again while Rudy and [[Amber]] waited for [[Johnny]]'s smoke break, clearly needling Rudy about "showing up where trouble's brewing." Confirmed via [[Johnny]] that Humboldt has been loitering around that specific service station "more than usual" for a couple of weeks — watching for someone or something, not just idle. Got called away by a radio dispatch from [[Detective Marcus Cole|Marcus Cole]] before the confrontation escalated; unclear whether Marcus's timing was deliberate cover for Rudy or a genuine coincidence.

**Working theory (per the GM's framing during play, not yet confirmed in a scene):** Humboldt's animosity toward Rudy is tied to Rudy's time in the Marines — the implication is that Rudy did something, in that period, that divided Humboldt's family. Not yet explained further.
