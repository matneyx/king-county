A county official — blond, receding widow's-peak hairline going grey at the temples. Came to [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] years before the events of [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]]'s present day, shortly after Rudy first bought [[Shepherd's Inn]] — the first case Rudy recalls taking as a hunter-for-hire in King County.

His son and the son's fiancée were kidnapped; a ransom note demanded he bring money alone to a clearing called [[Boot Knife]]. He complied, but the police (who insisted on hidden backup despite his instructions) may have spooked whoever was supposed to collect it — no one showed. The next morning, a second envelope arrived at his office: "I said come alone. Try again," along with a severed adult finger (corrected 2026-07-14 — earlier notes mistakenly attributed a child's finger to this case; that detail actually belongs to [[Jeff Speno]]'s envelope in the present day, per a careful re-read of the manuscript's confusingly-transitioned flashback).

Rudy told him he'd find his son. The outcome of this case is not shown on the page — only recalled as backstory while Rudy investigates [[Jeff Speno]]'s near-identical kidnapping in the present day. The same "come alone / failed attempt / severed finger" pattern recurring with Speno implies Dawes's case was never truly resolved, or that whoever was responsible is active again.

**The "thank you gift":** Rudy's Shepherd's Inn holds legal immunity from county code enforcement — described as a favor from a county official in gratitude for Rudy saving his son's life. Almost certainly refers to Dawes, though not stated explicitly on the page.
