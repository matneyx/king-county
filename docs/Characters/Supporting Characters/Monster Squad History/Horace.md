A neighborhood kid who befriended [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] over the summer before either of them started school together — round-faced, easygoing, never treated Rudy like the "dangerous new kid" everyone else assumed he was. Rudy didn't hear the other kids call him "Fat Kid" until weeks after they'd already become friends, and it pissed him off when he did.

Invited Rudy into the [[The Monster Squad (group)|Monster Club]] right as the school year started — nervous about it, half-expecting to be laughed at.

**Confirmed scenes:**
- Got in trouble with [[Sean]] in the principal's office for drawing monster pictures in science class — Rudy, despite never actually having been in trouble himself before moving, talked Horace down with an invented line about permanent records: "It doesn't matter. Your permanent record is a myth. I've been to the principal's office hundreds of times, and none of that stuff followed me here."
- Present with Rudy in the Vampire's mansion when Wolfman was there — kicked Wolfman in the nards.
- **His level-up moment**, fighting off one of the Vampire's brides: cocking a shotgun and declaring, "My name," he says, cocking the shotgun in his hands, "is Horace!"

**Fate, confirmed (2026-07-22, decided live — deliberate homage, not subverted):** Not hunted, not killed by anything connected to the case. Horace wanted no part of what came after — he wanted to be safe, and ignorance was bliss, so he pulled away from Rudy as Rudy got more obsessive about the whole thing, well before Rudy ever joined the Marines. He moved away, lost touch with everyone from that life, and years later died of pneumonia as an adult — an ordinary, unconnected death. Rudy only learned about it much later, secondhand, the same distant way anyone who'd known of him might have — no one left who'd kept in touch to pass the news along directly. Deliberately mirrors the real fate of Brent Chalem, the actor who played Horace in *The Monster Squad* (1987), who died of pneumonia as an adult — Dave's own choice to honor that rather than write around it.

**Estrangement from Rudy, confirmed (2026-07-22, decided live):** Not a single dramatic falling-out — a gradual drift, spread out over time. Horace wanted normalcy; Rudy was becoming the "obsessed hunter" type even as a teenager, and that obsession is what actually pushed people away, not some external threat picking them off one by one.
