[[Leanne]]'s son. Full name Caleb Thomas, age six. Believed to live in a trailer park beyond the plow line in [[King County, Colorado|King County]].

**Surname and age discrepancies, resolved (2026-07-15, decided live, grill-canon session):** The [[Character-Driven Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Character-Driven alternate outline]] called him "Caleb Raines," and the [[Mystery Crime Thriller Alternate Outline (What Wasn't and What Was)|Mystery Crime Thriller alternate outline]] gave his age as five in its prologue draft — both cascade-resolved along with the outline-primacy decision (see [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]]). "Caleb Thomas," age six, is the established, canonical name and age.

**Confirmed reveal:** Caleb was never born — he's a grief-born thoughtform. His "disappearance" is the central mystery of [[What Wasn't and What Was]], but the true mystery is that he never existed as a physical child. The emotional truth and the narrative truth are meant to be the same: he didn't exist, but he mattered.

**The real mechanism (confirmed):** [[Leanne]] really was pregnant. She borrowed an unregistered car to clear her head before the baby came and was in a solo car crash — admitted to a hospital as a Jane Doe (mild concussion, internal bleeding), pregnant on arrival, no longer pregnant on discharge. She left without signing out. The real baby was cremated and placed in an unmarked community plot. "Caleb" — walking, talking, age six, going to school — is the thoughtform Leanne's grief and belief built around that real loss.

**Physical details:** Wears a hoodie with ears. Has a personal copy of a Berenstain Bears book in his room with a broken spine (distinct from — and in tension with — the library copy that was donated and never checked out; see the discrepancy note on [[What Wasn't and What Was]]).

**Gothic sensory detail (offered, not confirmed adopted):** His room is described as smelling like wet leaves and milk teeth.

**Evidence clues supporting the reveal (to be planted through the story):**
- Rudy finds Caleb's shoe, still warm — the one purely physical, objectively strange clue.
- A torn picture frame with a faded wall-shadow outline, but no photo inside.
- A box of toys labeled with a name that isn't used anymore.
- A prenatal medical chart that has no postnatal follow-up.
- A kindergarten school application that was never processed.
- His favorite book at the [[King County Library]] was donated by Leanne years ago — and has never been checked out.
- The missing child report was never actually filed, or was filed and withdrawn, or the case number doesn't exist anymore.
- A baby book and "the drawing," kept by Rudy as the case unravels — whose drawing this is was never actually specified in the source material. It could be a drawing of/by Caleb, or it could be the same [[Phoebe|Ashley]] crayon drawing already established in Rudy's keepsake drawer, carried through Act 2 and placed on Caleb's grave in 2.8 as a symbol of grief transferred from one lost child to another. The latter reading is thematically stronger but unconfirmed — worth deciding deliberately rather than assuming.
- The Jane Doe hospital record showing Leanne pregnant on arrival, not pregnant on discharge.
- His unmarked grave in a community plot.

**Doubt, not disbelief, confirmed (2026-07-16, decided live — from "Westernizing What Wasn't"):** The people around Caleb doubt him — they hesitate, they misremember, they contradict themselves — but that's a meaningfully different, softer thing than actively disbelieving in him. Doubt leaves room open; outright disbelief would close it. This distinction is the mechanical hinge for Caleb's intermittent on-page appearances: he's able to flicker into being at moments of peak collective belief (a candlelight vigil being the clearest example) precisely because doubt hasn't hardened into disbelief yet. Once belief curdles all the way into disbelief, he can't show up at all — which is also why he doesn't reappear in Act III until the mystery is actually resolved and Leanne chooses to believe again.

**Instability of his name:** Adults are hesitant or contradict each other about him — "I could've sworn she had a kid," "Didn't she come back from Salt Lake with a baby?", "I thought his name was Jacob." None of it reads as suspicious in the moment — just the natural fog of small-town memory.

**Confirmed name-flicker:** The specific alternate name is Jacob Thomas. At Caleb's vigil, someone calls him "Jacob" aloud and Leanne corrects them instantly; in that same moment, Rudy sees the "Caleb Thomas" missing poster flicker to read "Jacob Thomas" before reverting — noticed only by him. This is meant to echo again in Act 2, when someone calls him Jacob and doesn't realize they've said it.
