A road companion [[Jamie|Moss]] traveled with for a time. Age uncertain — maybe 17, could've been 20; Moss never asked. Road-worn, cocky, "too clever for his own damn good," talked like "a podcast host, all confidence and bullshit wisdom." Wore a neon orange hoodie with "SECURITY" ironed on the back (a joke), always inside out. Called Moss "Cavekid" — said like a compliment, like they were carved out of myth (possibly hinting Rye sensed something old/mythic about Moss, never confirmed).

**The dawn rule and what happened:** Moss and Rye caught out together in Nebraska, camped under a half-collapsed boxcar with a can of Sterno and a jar of peaches. Moss refused to hop a train at dawn ("We wait. Dawn's bad luck" / "It's when I came back"). Rye mocked the rule ("your faerie bedtime rules... it's a goddamn sunrise, not a banshee") and tried to hop the grainer anyway. A railroad bull appeared without warning; in the chaos (a shout, a flashlight, a bang that hit metal not flesh) Rye slipped and his leg went under the wheel. His last words to Moss: "You were right. Cavekid... you were right." He didn't die immediately, but Moss fled before the ambulance arrived.

**Aftermath:** His hoodie was soaked in blood in the immediate aftermath. Moss never saw Rye again — fate unknown; "maybe he's alive, limping through Denver on a peg leg. Maybe not." This is the origin of [[Jamie|Moss]]'s dawn curse. Moss won't speak Rye's name aloud, and leaves orange scraps (torn hoodie bits, burnt caution tape) tied to fenceposts near trainyards — a warning for others, a shrine. At dawn, Moss sometimes hears his voice: "Just ride, Cavekid. Just go." Thematic closing line: "Dawn still means blood on the rails."
