**One of the creek kids, resolved (2026-07-15, decided live, grill-canon session — this is also the "Ben" previously flagged as possible ChatGPT contamination):** Ben is the kid who meets [[Jamie]] at the tree line and explains the local kids' fear and rituals around the Huntsman, whom the creek kids call **"the Hollowman."** He's sneaking out early that morning specifically to nail his baby sister's baby tooth to the tree near the Hedge opening — a folk-charm meant to keep her safe from the Hollowman. Deliberately kept as the same character rather than introducing a new NPC to fill the role — Dave's own standing preference is to reuse an existing character over adding one when the story allows it.

**Ben is also the vault's previously-unnamed "second missing kid," confirmed (2026-07-16, decided live — from "Westernizing What Wasn't"):** After roughly eight years failing to catch Jamie directly, [[The Huntsman]] changes tactics and kidnaps Ben instead — exploiting the one thing Jamie won't run from, someone else in danger. This forces Rudy and Jamie into **Arcadia** (confirmed, not the Hedge — see [[The Huntsman]]) to get him back, which is where the Huntsman is finally killed. **Timing, confirmed same session:** this happens at the **end of Act II**, functioning as the book's red herring/false climax — Rudy and the reader are primed to assume the Huntsman took Ben the same way it's always pursued Jamie, when really it's an unrelated tragedy from Caleb's belief-based existence fading (see [[Caleb]]). Rescuing Ben is also the mechanism by which Rudy and Jamie learn Caleb was never really there (see [[What Wasn't and What Was]]) — the real, rescued child is proof of what Caleb's absence couldn't be. This ties Ben directly into both the A and B plots rather than leaving him a background character.

**Confirmed sequencing (2026-07-18, decided live, grill-canon session):** Ben meets Jamie at the tree line (above) → sometime later, the Huntsman's "Near Miss" beat happens (see [[What Wasn't and What Was]]) → after that, Ben goes missing. His disappearance is what moves "the Huntsman took Caleb" from a maybe to a near-certainty in Rudy's read of the case — deliberately, since the Huntsman *wants* Moss to know it was the one who took him. There's no ambiguity or misdirection on that specific point within the B plot itself; the misdirection is entirely about what it means for the A plot (Caleb). **Near Miss location, confirmed (per "Making What Wasn't Scooby-Doo," full data export):** the Near Miss beat needs its own location, separate from this tree-line meeting — deliberate narrative separation so it doesn't read as the same scene once Ben disappears. **Founding relic and timeline, confirmed (2026-07-22, per "King County Chronicles ChatGPT consolidatation"):** The tree's first nailed tooth was [[Jamie|Jamie/Moss]]'s own — knocked loose tripping on the way out of the Hedge, too old by then (~12) to still be losing baby teeth naturally. All of the kids' actual lore around the tree was invented after Jamie's return, 8 years ago — meaning there's only 8 years' worth of teeth actually nailed into the bark, not centuries of them despite how old the ritual might feel to the kids who grew up with it.

**How the illusion holds up for adults, confirmed (same source):** Older siblings quietly swap the tooth for a coin before or after the actual parents do the tooth-fairy routine — each parent assumes the other one did it, or forgot. Adults keep believing in an ordinary tooth fairy while the kids secretly maintain the real ward underneath it.

**The tooth-nailing rule, confirmed (2026-07-18, decided live, grill-canon session):** The rule of the charm isn't that a kid protects themself — someone *else* has to nail a kid's baby tooth to the tree on their behalf. Ben has a few of his own baby teeth left that haven't been placed yet, since he can't do it himself. Floated, not fully locked: Jamie/Moss might be the one who ends up nailing Ben's tooth to the tree for him. **Confirmed same session:** this rule isn't something Arcadia or the Huntsman designed — it's a binding the Huntsman picked up entirely from the creek kids' own superstition, and the Huntsman is genuinely, "weirdly" bound by it anyway. See [[The Huntsman]].

**The bloody molar clue, confirmed (2026-07-18, decided live, grill-canon session):** When the Huntsman takes Ben, it leaves a bloody baby molar under his pillow — a fucked-up tooth fairy, deliberately. This is a real, plantable clue with two distinct readings that split exactly along the case's supernatural/mundane fault line (see [[Noir and Hardboiled Style Guide|the "a clue must lead somewhere" principle]]): Rudy's read is procedural — he wants to confirm whether Ben had actually lost that tooth recently, since kids commonly lie to their parents about losing teeth to collect tooth-fairy money, so it might just be a coincidence worth ruling out. Jamie's read is immediate and certain — Moss knows exactly what the tooth means the second they see it, tied directly to the tooth-nailing charm's rules (see above): the Huntsman isn't just taking Ben, it's mocking the very ward meant to stop it. **Discovery mechanism, confirmed (per "Making What Wasn't Scooby-Doo," full data export — Dave's own reasoning):** Dave was explicit that this can't be a clue Rudy or Jamie go digging for — Ben already told Moss directly that he had a loose molar he wanted nailed to the tree once it fell out, so the reader already has the information. The tooth under the pillow is found because Ben's mother, grieving and looking for anything of her son's to hold onto while Rudy questions her, lifts his pillow herself. Rudy then confirms with her that Ben hadn't mentioned losing that tooth. The clue isn't uncovered by investigative cleverness — it's stumbled into, which is what makes it land as devastating rather than procedural.

**Confirmed climax beat (2026-07-18, decided live, grill-canon session):** When Rudy and Jamie go into Arcadia looking for missing kids, Ben is the only one they actually find — and Ben confirms directly that he was the only child he ever saw there. The Huntsman may also confirm this, possibly mockingly, hinting that Caleb "wasn't his to take" (or something similarly suggestive of Caleb's belief-based nature) without giving the full reveal away. Exact integration with the pre-existing 2.6 "empty clearing" beat left deliberately unresolved for now — see the flag on [[What Wasn't and What Was]].
