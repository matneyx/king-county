**Role in Setting:** [[Jamie|Moss]]'s drug source — a fence operating out of [[Red Bridge, Colorado|Red Bridge]], a small, run-down pass-through town off the Shepherd-Jackson Highway.

**Origin, confirmed (2026-07-22, decided live):** Reused from Dave's own past *Hunter: the Vigil* tabletop game — Christie Reese is the "Aloof Fence" NPC from the published adventure *The Harvesters* (see `docs/Themes and Notes/World of Darkness Resources/World of Darkness - The Harvesters.pdf`), and the character his players actually met and dealt with in that earlier, unrelated campaign. Reused here as a King County NPC, not necessarily importing that adventure's own plot (a kidnapping by a cult of thugs) wholesale — just the character herself and her established M.O.

**The game itself, confirmed (2026-07-22):** That campaign was run for Dave's own long-running tabletop group, "Poker Night" (an inside joke — one player didn't want to admit to family they were playing D&D, so they said poker instead). This same group's actual play-through of *The Harvesters* is also the origin of the farmhouse at [[Boot Knife]] — see that page and [[The Three Generations Ritual]] for how much of it carries over.

**Per the source material — background and personality:** A fence, not a dealer exclusively — she moves whatever's hard to get, drugs included, across a wide territory (anything within several hours' drive), without actually knowing the town she operates out of especially well. Prudence as her defining virtue (assess a situation before exploiting it), Greed as her vice (the payoff is the best feeling she has). Average-looking in a way that reads as sexy in the right light and unremarkable in daylight; tough but visibly a little desperate — leans in slightly when she talks, has a needy quality in her eyes she can't quite hide. Smells of cigarettes and fruit-scented shampoo; long auburn hair she brushes out of her face out of nervous habit; slightly taller than average, thin, multiple ear piercings, a fake sapphire stud in her right nostril. Keeps people at a distance on purpose — real connections are just something else to lose — which also means she sometimes misses things she should've caught.

**Her M.O., per the source material:** Meets clients somewhere low-key and recurring (a donut shop in the original material, where a small bribe buys the owner's silence) rather than wherever the actual deal happens. Doesn't carry product to first meetings. Picks the exchange site herself — someplace "cheap, out of the way, and virtually abandoned most of the time." In the source material, that's an old motel down a quiet stretch of road with a rest stop across from it — which is exactly the shape of the location already confirmed for Moss's supply runs (see [[The Huntsman]]).

**Adapted for King County (2026-07-22, decided live):** Whether Christie's full kidnapping-plot backstory from *The Harvesters* is canon here, or she's just reused as a standing NPC Moss deals with, is not yet decided — treat the adventure's own plot as background flavor/available material, not automatically imported.
