Employee at [[LeGrande Station]] in [[Shepherd Junction, Colorado|Shepherd Junction]] since age 16. Hispanic — confirmed in [[The Prodigal Son (A King County Chronicle)|The Prodigal Son]], the first place her ethnicity is specified anywhere in this vault. Works graveyard shifts while putting herself through nursing school (CNA track) — [[Nathan Hale|Hale]] has lined up a CNA job lead for her at a home in Jensen. Resourceful: once fixed her own car with a zip tie and a butter knife.

**Relationship to [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]]:** His grounding force in [[What Wasn't and What Was]]. Where Rudy reads every hardship as a sign or consequence of the supernatural, Jenn refuses to mythologize pain — she's lived through real hardship (a bad hospital, a cold doctor, financial strain) with no curse behind any of it. She's not resistant to the supernatural so much as irrelevant to its rules; it can't tempt or seduce her because she's already survived hell without needing it to be magic.

Her non-magical read on events is played straight as correct, not naive — she calls things "bad luck" that Rudy knows are something else (a spell gone wrong), and both of them are right. Rudy sees a weird shadow move and reaches for iron; Jenn just flips the breaker and grumbles about mice. His trauma is cosmic; hers is clinical, institutional, financial, intimately human. "She believes in people. He believes in what happens when people look away too long."

Her view of King County, in contrast to Rudy's "thin place" framing: "A dead-end stretch of nowhere with too much quiet at night, bills that don't quit, and pain that no one talks about because everyone's got their own."

Grounding line to Rudy: "No, Rudy. This one's not on you. It's just life being cruel."

Foreshadowing (unconfirmed): described as "unaware she's being dragged toward something old and ugly" — implies she isn't yet aware of her own entanglement in the supernatural side of the story.

**Confirmed age (per "Rudy Jenn Age Gap," full data export):** 26–27 in Book 1 (September 2014) — Dave's own relieved reaction after running the math: "I was super afraid Jenn was gonna be like 21 or something. 26-27 is a perfect age for her." This puts roughly 16–17 years between her and Rudy, using the "half-your-age-plus-seven" rule as the deliberate benchmark for "just eyebrow-raising, not outright gross." Astrological profile floated and built on rather than rejected: Virgo Sun, Scorpio Moon, Capricorn Rising, birthday **September 9, 1987**.

**Confirmed romance-arc dynamic:** Jenn pursues Rudy, not the other way around — all of the age/danger concerns are already out in the open before their first kiss, since Rudy needs to feel she genuinely understands the risk before he lets her in. Her own line, hinting at unspecified past loss (possibly her miscarriage — see [[What Wasn't and What Was]]'s Jenn/car-accident clue): *"I've buried more friends than I've kissed. I held my own blood in my hands when I was nineteen. You think you're the only one with ghosts?"*

**Confirmed first-kiss beat:** After Rudy says, "I've buried more people than I've loved" (a deliberate correction from an earlier draft's "kissed" — Rudy's a one-night-stand/occasional-sex-worker-client kind of guy, so "kissed" undersold how rare *love* actually is for him), Jenn kisses him first: *"I know I can't fix that... but hopefully that improves the odds."*

Brainstormed future plot beat: Jenn eventually gets a glimpse of the supernatural truth herself. Craft note from the brainstorm — play it quiet and unavoidable, not a big horror reveal, since it shatters a boundary she didn't know she'd been walking. (In tension with a separately drafted, explicit confirmation elsewhere in the same broader conversation that "Jenn doesn't know about the supernatural. Never will if Rudy has anything to say about it" — not reconciled.)

**The Hollow Creek rumor, resolved (2026-07-16, decided live, grill-canon session):** Hale tells her a rumor that Rudy shot and killed a man near "Hollow Creek" years ago — no body, no charges, just someone who stopped showing up. Confirmed true: a young-generation vampire kill that came out looking human since young vampires don't decay on final death the way old ones do (see [[Rudy Williams]]). She doesn't flinch either way: "Rudy's never lied to me. Not once... You know how rare that is?" and "I'll take dangerous and honest over clean and fake any day."

**The "bear" framing (brainstormed, not confirmed as adopted dialogue):** Riffing on the "woman alone with a strange man vs. a bear" meme — the idea is that Jenn would choose Rudy over the bear, specifically because his danger is honest and known, unlike most men's.

**On Rudy's past, when pressed:** She once guessed he might be "a war vet. Or a burnout preacher. Or an ex-cop. Or one of those guys who faked their death and started over in a town with no stoplight." Rudy's answer: "All of the above, give or take." She never asked outright — reasoning that if he wanted her to know, he'd tell her, and either way she already knows what matters: that he's not a bad man.
