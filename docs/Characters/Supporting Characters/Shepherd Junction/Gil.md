Grey-bearded, leather-clad biker — roughly twenty years older than [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]], with a similar depth of world-weary wisdom in his eyes. A regular at [[Shepherd's Inn]], quiet by nature, communicates mostly in grunts and raised glasses.

A recurring "one more for the road" ritual with Rudy makes up most of the Inn's actual daily business. Recently became a grandfather. Trusted enough that Rudy leaves him watching the bar (with a shotgun under the counter, only half-joking about using it on anyone trying to steal a drink) when he needs to run an errand — Gil left a stack of bills on the counter and went home rather than actually cover for him, though, so how far that trust really extends is a little unclear.

**Legal name (confirmed 2026-07-16, decided live):** Robert Gilbert — "Gil" isn't short for a first name at all. Rudy only knows this because Gil paid with a check once. He went by "Gilbert" in the service, which other sailors shortened to "Gil," and it stuck for life.

**Full backstory, confirmed (2026-07-16, decided live):** A 22-year Navy veteran — a Navy diver — who then drove long-haul trucking for another 20-plus years until he could retire. Married during his Navy years, divorced at some point after; had a few kids, all of whom settled down in King County — which is why Gil always used King County as his home base even during his long-haul years, driving back specifically to see his kids and grandkids. He's never missed a birthday, graduation, or major holiday. A good guy.

**Biker identity:** Not a 1%er — riding was a weekender hobby during his working years, and only became a major part of his identity once he retired and had the time for it to be one. His road name in biker circles is **"Gillman"** — half a play on his Navy diver background, half a nod to "Gillman," the IP-safe name *The Monster Squad* used in its own credits for its Creature-from-the-Black-Lagoon analogue (consistent with this vault's established practice of borrowing that film's naming conventions — see [[The Monster Squad (Movie)]]). Rudy and the Inn regulars still just call him "Gil."

**An "armchair Hunter":** Knows the lore, knows the talk, has never actually done Hunter work — he's aware the supernatural world is out there without ever having engaged with it directly. Rudy doesn't know whether Gil has kept up his old military training at all, but suspects he has, given how active Gil still is in Hunter circles despite never having hunted anything himself.

**What Gil doesn't know:** Rudy doesn't talk about his own military service, so Gil almost certainly doesn't know about Rudy's dishonorable discharge. Dave's own read: Gil would probably understand and look past it if Rudy actually explained — but it's an open question whether Rudy would ever take that chance.
