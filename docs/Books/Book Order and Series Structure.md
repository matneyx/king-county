Overview of planned/in-progress novels set in [[King County, Colorado|King County]], using characters and events from the solo RPG sessions as source material.

Series order is still emerging as brainstorming continues.

**Confirms the series shares one continuity:** since this campaign is numbered alongside [[What Wasn't and What Was]] rather than treated as a separate, unrelated use of the setting, the "who is she" successor question (see [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]]) is very likely one puzzle to reconcile — [[Amber]] (confirmed via Book 3) and [[Jenn-with-two-Ns|Jenn]] (theorized via a brainstormed Book 4 letter) can't just both be true in separate unconnected stories the way "different campaign" framing would have allowed. Worth deciding deliberately which one it actually is, or how they relate (e.g., could Jenn's theorized letter simply be superseded/outdated brainstorming from before Amber was introduced in Book 3).

**A third, independently-drafted 6-book arc with actual titles — "The Rudy Williams Chronicles" (per "Supernatural Horror Publishers Guide," full data export, found on a fresh-eyes pass) — yet another data point in the same recurring renumbering conflict:**
- **Book 1 — *What Wasn't and What Was*.** Theme: "Guilt isn't the ghost — memory is."
- **Book 2 — *The Pattern of Flesh*.** Flashbacks to Rudy's first failed case against [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]]; ends with Mickey watching Rudy from a distance. Theme: "You can't outrun the past. You can only choose where it catches you."
- **Book 3 — untitled.** [[Aaron Mabey|Doc]], Rudy's one surviving military tie, resurfaces Changed and hunted; Rudy must choose between putting him down or standing by him. Theme: "Loyalty is a kind of madness. But so is mercy."
- **Book 4 — sequel to *The Pattern of Flesh*.** Mickey targets Rudy directly and personally. Theme: "When you open the door to monsters, they don't leave. They live in the hallway."
- **Book 5 — the California Incident book.** Rudy and [[Phoebe]] face the vampire from his childhood together; Rudy is forced into a leadership role for the first time. Theme: "You can face the monster, or you can become the man who taught others to fight."
- **Book 6 — the finale.** Mickey returns one final time; **Rudy dies**, on his own terms. Theme: "You don't escape the curse. You just choose who carries it next."
- **Confirmed Book 7 candidates (two different ones floated in the same chat, not reconciled with each other):** (a) A spinoff/novella centered on Ashley. (b) A protégé character — an Inn employee who starts as an errand-runner/quiet observer while Rudy's away and inherits Shepherd's Inn (and the series) after his death, explicitly tied by Dave to "the play-by-post where Marcus may have turned to the dark side" — very likely connects to the vault's existing, unresolved "Who is she?" successor question (Amber vs. Jenn) rather than being a third candidate. See [[Rudy Williams]] — Who Is "She"? in [[Development Notes & Unresolved Questions]].

Flagged as an open question rather than invented — it may exist in a conversation outside this export, in a chat that didn't get algorithmically flagged as King-County-relevant, or Dave may be thinking of a different session/notes document entirely.

**Another confirmed-by-Dave 6-book arc, predating the direct Book 3 = Clara McKenzie confirmation above (per "Astrology Birthday Guess," full data export) — yet another data point in the same recurring renumbering conflict, not a resolution of it:**
- **Book 1** = [[What Wasn't and What Was]] (implied, not restated directly in this chat).
- **Book 2** = [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] pursuing blood magic/immortality, framed by Dave as "another kid case" for Rudy.
- **Book 3** = Something dangerous enough that [[Aaron Mabey|Doc]] seeks Rudy out himself, rather than the reverse. Also confirmed: this is where the [[Jenn-with-two-Ns|Jenn]]/Rudy relationship "will be a real thing."
- **Book 4** = Mickey actively targets Rudy, dragging [[Phoebe]] into it. [[Jamie|Jamie/Moss]] also returns here — "not long term," and Dave specifically likes the idea of an earlier, off-page reappearance before this official return: Jamie sneaks into [[Shepherd's Inn]] during a Rudy stakeout, showers, and leaves before Rudy gets home.
- **Book 5** = The follow-up/conclusion to [[The California Incident]], with Phoebe as its driving force rather than a background element.
- **Book 6** = Series finale, full cast. **Mickey kills Rudy** — Mickey "wins" regardless of whether someone else kills Mickey in the process. Dave explicitly confirmed three named survivors: [[Jamie|Jamie/Moss]], [[Jenn-with-two-Ns|Jenn]], and [[Nathan Hale]].

This aligns with (and reinforces) the standalone [[Mickey Two-Dice]]/[[The Vampire]] confirmed endpoint from "Vampire Population Ratio" — Mickey diablerizing the returned Abyss-touched vampire and killing Rudy — suggesting that specific endpoint really is meant to be Book 6, even though neither chat cross-references the other directly.

**Speculative Book 3+ tone scaffolding (from "Werewolves and Spirit Worlds," full data export — explicitly NOT locked in; Dave's own closing words: "I don't need to figure Book 3 out until Book 2 is done" / "I don't NEED any of this now but it's good to think about it"):** Predates the direct 2026-07-14 confirmation above that Book 3 is the Clara McKenzie campaign — this chat instead envisioned a numbered "Book 3" centered on rescuing [[Aaron Mabey|Doc]] from a decaying, trauma-born otherworld nicknamed **"The Rusting Place"** (Silent Hill/Constantine-flavored), with an inserted "Book 2.5: The Clara Disappearance." Given Dave's later, direct confirmation that Clara McKenzie IS Book 3 outright (no ".5"), this whole numbering needs revisiting — the Doc-rescue story, if kept, would now land at Book 4 or later, unnumbered. **Confirmed tone rule regardless of renumbering:** Rudy should not be actively, directly hunted as a major theme before whichever book follows the Doc-rescue story — any harm before then should read as collateral/coincidental, not targeted, so that book's "the hunter becomes prey" shift lands hard. **Also floated for a later book (explicitly cosmic horror):** the Abyss returns, and Rudy assembles with [[Jamie|Moss]], Ashley (see [[Phoebe]]), and possibly Doc and [[Mickey Two-Dice|Mickey]] to face it.
- **Explicitly discarded from this brainstorm:** a "Fetch" version of Doc (a false, memory-virus double living a normal life in King County) and a "Rudy is immune to supernatural memory-erasure" trait — Dave rejected both directly: "I'm still not sold on the Fetch or Rudy's immaculate memory, either. This could be another rescue mission, like in Book 1, except we know for sure there's something or someone worth rescuing." Treat the whole Fetch/Project-Valkyrie/Department-of-Truth angle as abandoned, not canon.
- **Book 5's cosmic-horror identity, confirmed (not discarded) in the same chat, added on a 2026-07-16 fresh-eyes pass:** Dave's own words: "Book 5 is definitely cosmic horror." The Abyss is defined there as a "negative story" rather than a void — not absence but corruption, not just forgetting but rewriting, something that "eats the veil and tells you you were never there to see it"; it doesn't kill, it un-narrates. Each assembling character has a distinct thing they fear losing to it: Rudy fears losing the people he couldn't save, his memories, his usefulness; Moss fears losing their name; Ashley fears losing her rationality and selfhood; Doc fears his scars being rewritten as fiction; Mickey fears losing control. Same chat's per-book tone-calibration table, offered as scaffolding rather than a locked structure: Book 1 (psychological/Hedge/mystery horror, human-level truth vs. denial), Book 2 (supernatural/ritual/personal horror, moral decay), a "Book 2.5" Clara-disappearance bridge (grounded tragedy, no clean ending — superseded now that Clara McKenzie is directly confirmed as Book 3, not 2.5), Book 3/Doc-rescue (existential/trauma/haunted-house horror, guilt and loyalty), Book 4 (suspense/high-stakes, the hunter becomes prey), Book 5 (cosmic horror, identity and belief itself breaking down).
- **Unconnected antagonist-concept pool, not yet tied to any specific book (from "Existential Threats to Werewolves," full data export):** A generic Hunter: the Vigil/Werewolf-flavored cosmic-horror entity nicknamed "the Coil That Whispers" — a belief/oath-corrupting parasite that turns communities into their own worst instincts rather than fighting them directly — was extensively brainstormed as one option for a future antagonist. Dave's own close: "this has promise. I need to chew on it a bit." Not tied to King County specifically, no character names carried over — pure concept-pool material, worth revisiting only if a later book needs this flavor of threat.

**Brainstormed "Book 4" beat (very rough, from the "Escalating Tensions Rewrite" chat):** Implies at least four books are envisioned in the series, though book 4 has no other details anywhere in this vault beyond this single beat (book 3 is now the Clara McKenzie campaign above). The idea: the [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] / [[Jenn-with-two-Ns|Jenn]] / [[Nathan Hale|Hale]] triangle comes to a head in an "EVACUATION" scene — something catastrophic is happening in King County, and Rudy sends Hale to get Jenn to safety while he goes to face the threat alone, telling Hale "What I do, Nathan" and "Yeah. Probably. But you won't. Not if you hurry." Hale ends up having to protect Jenn himself against a complication along the way, and steps up despite having none of Rudy's training. Afterward (Rudy dead, or "his body's gone cold" — left as an open choice), Hale receives Rudy's will/letter — see [[Rudy Williams]] and [[Nathan Hale]] for the full text. Framed as the resolution of "a triangle of trust, forged in fire": Rudy → Jenn is trust without confession; Jenn → Hale is steady loyalty without romance; Hale → Rudy moves from disgust and resentment to genuine understanding.
