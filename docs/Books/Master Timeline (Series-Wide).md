## Macro-Act Structure (confirmed reading, 2026-07-22, decided live)
The 9-slot book structure (8 numbered books + the unnumbered Mickey/Angel side book, per [[Book Order and Series Structure]]) reads cleanly as **3 macro-acts of 3 books each** — the whole series as a 3-act play, each act itself a 3-act structure:

- **Macro-Act I — The Mystery Years:** Book 1 (*What Wasn't and What Was*) → Book 2 (*A Pattern of Blood*) → Book 3 (*Clara*)
- **Macro-Act II — The Siege:** Book 4 (*Doc*) → Mickey and Angel side book → Book 5 (*Mickey Targets Rudy*)
- **Macro-Act III — The Reckoning:** Book 6 (*Silent Hill Haunted House*) → Book 7 (*Abyss Reopens*) → Book 8 (*Avengers Assemble; Rudy Dies*)

Not yet confirmed: whether Dave wants these macro-act names, or just the 3x3 grouping itself. Treat the names above as placeholders.

## Prehistory (before Book 1, off-page)
- **1986, The California Incident:** The Monster Squad (Rudy, Phoebe, Sean, Horace) stops the Vampire's attempt to bring the Abyss through. The Promethean pushes the Vampire into the breach and follows him in; Phoebe seals it behind both of them via a spontaneous Awakening. See [[The California Incident]], [[The Vampire]], [[The Promethean]].
- **~1918-1920s, Castle Dell, GA:** Michael Turino (Mickey) arrives, is Embraced by Missy, diablerizes Harv the same night, later diablerizes Kayla and his own sire, survives a Blood Hunt via the "Gnawed" decoy. See [[Mickey Two-Dice]].
- **~1990-91, Kuwait:** Rudy's unit, including Doc (Aaron Mabey), is wiped out by Doc's First Change. Rudy goes AWOL hunting him for six months, is dishonorably discharged. See [[Aaron Mabey]].
- **~8 years before Book 1:** Jamie/Moss escapes the Hedge, is rescued by Rudy from the Huntsman. See [[Jamie]].
- **Some years before Book 1:** Executive Dawes's case — the original Three Generations Ritual, Rudy's first King County hunt. See [[The Three Generations Ritual]].

## Mickey's book-spanning ambition (confirmed threads pulled together)
Mickey's arc is one continuous throughline, not book-siloed: seek immortality without becoming a monster or losing himself (Book 2-era framing) → operate through Angel as reconnaissance while quietly expanding his reach (Books 3-4, the side book) → escalate to open siege against everyone Rudy loves once subtlety stops working (Book 5) → acquire the power to actually win by diablerizing the returned Abyss-touched vampire (Book 7) → the final confrontation, where his own besetting flaw (brilliant planner, terrible improviser, especially under grief or close to victory) is what finally costs him everything (Book 8). See [[Mickey Two-Dice]] for the full character breakdown.

## Hale's book-spanning ambition (confirmed threads pulled together)
Also continuous: property-feud antagonist over Shepherd's Inn (Book 1) → floated political arc, running for County Commissioner, unknowingly playing the same "sanitize the battlefield" role the Camarilla plays against the Sabbat (placement not yet pinned to a specific book) → surrogate-father protectiveness over Jenn throughout → a drafted far-future scene where Rudy finally levels with him and asks him to keep Jenn safe rather than fight (see [[Nathan Hale]]) → confirmed as one of the named survivors through the series' end, potentially inheriting Shepherd's Inn per Rudy's will/letter. **Real gap:** Hale's Commissioner arc has never been placed in a specific book on this grid — worth pinning down.
