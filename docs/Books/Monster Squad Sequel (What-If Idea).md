A speculative "what if" idea — explicitly **not** part of [[What Wasn't and What Was]] or confirmed as a planned book. A spiritual sequel to [[The California Incident]] (King County's in-universe equivalent of [[The Monster Squad (Movie)]]): [[The Vampire]] returns from the Abyss, and [[Rudy Williams|Rudy]] needs an adult [[Phoebe]]'s help to end it for good.

**Real-world inspiration:** *Those Meddling Kids* by Edgar Cantero — a spiritual successor to Scooby-Doo about aging and trauma, cited as the tonal model for this idea (reckoning with the past rather than mocking it).

**Candidate titles:** *What Comes Back* or *The Thing in the Dark That Waited*.

**Hypothetical setup:** Strange murders echo the California Incident (victims staked, burned, or drained). Rudy investigates and finds signs he hoped never to see again. Someone sends him a photo of a girl holding a stuffed animal — her daughter, who looks just like Phoebe did. Complication: the vampire is back, but so is the Promethean/"Frankenstein" — not a foe this time, he remembers.

**Theme:** You can't bury monsters. And you can't bury what they made of you.

**Additional details from a separate capture of this same brainstorm** (full data export vs. an earlier direct copy-paste — likely two different branches of one long ChatGPT conversation, since the details don't fully overlap): Phoebe alternatively framed as "now a therapist," who dreams of the portal opening again and reaches out to Rudy, whom she hasn't seen in years. An additional story hook: "the Fetch of one of their old [Monster Squad] friends returns... but it's wrong" (a Changeling: The Lost "fetch" — a decoy left behind when someone is taken into Arcadia — applied to a Monster Squad character; an unusual cross-lore idea). More candidate titles floated in this version: "The Monster Still Lives," "Squad: Blood Oath," "The Abyss Gave Back," "What Came Back" (near-identical to "What Comes Back" above).

**Important conflict with established canon:** This idea imagines Phoebe as a functional adult with a daughter of her own — which directly conflicts with her established current state (catatonic, institutionalized in [[King County, Colorado|King County]]). Treat this entirely as a hypothetical alternate direction for her character, not a fact about her present condition. See [[Phoebe]].

**Vampire/Promethean lore that came out of this brainstorm (potentially useful regardless of whether this book ever gets written):**
- The Vampire's Abyss banishment was never a full death — see [[The Vampire]] for the "banished, not destroyed" clarification and speculative clan identity (Ventrue, possibly Lasombra).
- A hypothetical version where the Promethean returns as an ally, "he remembers" — see [[The Promethean]].
