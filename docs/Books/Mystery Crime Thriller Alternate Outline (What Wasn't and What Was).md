## 1.1 – Present the Crime (Prologue candidates)
Three unconfirmed options for depicting Caleb's disappearance directly as the prologue (an alternative to the Jamie-Hedge-exit prologue already in the main outline):
- **Omniscient/cinematic:** Pre-dawn blue light in the trailer. Caleb (described here as five years old — conflicts with the established age six) pads barefoot to the screen door; a whisper from the tree line; a shadow "like the absence of light"; the door creaks, the screen slams. Cut to black.
- **Caleb's POV:** Ordinary morning routine (breakfast, cartoons, toys) disrupted — the TV goes fuzzy, his name is whispered from outside, the trees seem closer than they should, he steps toward the creek and the Hedge as the world subtly shifts.
- **Leanne's POV (fragmented/unreliable):** A retrospective insert rather than a true prologue — she remembers coming home and feeling something was off, can't recall if she locked the door, finds an open toy box and a muddy footprint.

## 1.2 – Introduce the Sleuth (scene candidates)
Two unconfirmed staging options for Rudy's introduction/the inciting call (overlaps with the established Act 1.4/1.5):
- **At the Inn:** Marcus Cole calls quietly, "You still help people?" Rudy looks at a wall of old case photos, unsolved cases, yellowed newspaper clippings — maybe one in particular, a kid he didn't save, "the reason he doesn't sleep much anymore." Lights a cigarette: "Yeah. I'll take a look."
- **Someone comes to him:** Leanne or Jenn shows up; Rudy is cold and suspicious at first, then gets a faraway look as he starts forming theories. Tells her to go home, he'll look into it. Afterward digs out his old tools — cold iron, salt, "a charm he swore never to use again" (undeveloped — what charm, and why the vow?).
- Line: "He's the kind of guy who cares quietly — but fiercely."

## 3.4 – Final Confrontation: Into the Hollow (a fifth climax draft)
Rudy and Jamie track "the Hollow" (this outline's term — possibly an alternate name for [[The Hedge]], possibly a distinct sub-location, not reconciled) using Caleb's drawing, which depicts an iron arch — once overgrown, now exposed. Just before dawn; Jamie: "It's opening again." They bring offerings: candy, cold iron, memory. Inside, the world is wrong — trees bend the wrong way, the moon flickers between full and gone, the path changes when unwatched. Jamie walks confidently (shaped by this place); Rudy spots Caleb's shoe wedged in a root and takes the lead.

**Yet another Huntsman physical description (a fifth, distinct from the Gothic hat/shadow/mirror and the Detective Noir shape-shifter):** "a towering, faceless entity with antlers like dead trees and a cloak made of rotting leaves and stolen names." Caleb stands beside it, entranced, fading. The Huntsman speaks in echoes of Rudy's own doubts: "You never save them." / "You brought another broken child here." / "He was forgotten. You did not believe hard enough."

**New stakes rule:** Jamie warns that killing the Huntsman would be a mistake — "If you kill it, the Hollow eats what it has." Rudy instead drops the drawing (the same ambiguous object flagged on [[Caleb]]'s page — here specifically framed as "the drawing Caleb made after he vanished," an odd detail given Caleb's non-existence) and calls Caleb's name: "You're real. You were never just a story. Come home." The Huntsman lunges; Jamie intervenes, offering themselves.

**Two resolution options:**
- **Positive:** Rudy grabs Caleb, Jamie shouts "GO!," they break free as the arch closes, Jamie barely makes it through. Caleb wakes disoriented but safe; Rudy holds him: "You're good enough. Just like this."
- **Poetic/bittersweet (notably darker than any other draft — Rudy is the one lost, not Jamie):** Caleb escapes, but Rudy is pulled under. Jamie and Caleb survive; Jamie raises Caleb like a sibling. Jenn later receives a final letter: "Tell him I believed in him. Every day."

## 3.5 – Resolution of Subplot: Rudy Comes Home (a sixth epilogue draft)
Rudy returns to Shepherd's Inn — quiet, cold, familiar; stands in the doorway "like a ghost, but this time he's the one who came back." Jenn's left pie or leftovers; a thank-you note from Leanne and a Polaroid of Caleb smiling are on the counter. Rudy pulls the California Incident file, doesn't burn or file it away, writes on the front: "I did everything I could. That's enough." Possible closing letter: "There are always monsters. That won't change. But there are always kids worth saving, too. You just have to decide which one you believe in more." He stops calling it a failure when he talks about Jamie; laughs at Caleb's dumb jokes; starts journaling again, "preparing for the next time but not dreading it." Symbol of growth: repairs the Inn's loose stair, and puts up a new sign — "Safe Place. Warm Food. No Questions." — which Jamie paints. Final feeling: he doesn't forget the Hollow, doesn't need to; wakes without reaching for the bottle (another instance of the drinking characterization that's in tension with his established sober trait — see [[Rudy Williams]]); still dreams, but not just ghosts — sometimes memories, sometimes possibilities.
