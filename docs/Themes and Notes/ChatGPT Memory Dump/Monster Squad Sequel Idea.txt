This isn't important for this story but it's a what-if in regards to Rudy in general.

There's a book -- Those Meddling Kids by Edgar Cantero -- that's kind of a spiritual successor to Scooby Doo stories.

Rudy and Ashley are strongly inspired by Rudy and Phoebe from The Monster Squad. "The California Incident" is basically The Monster Squad.

Would it be appropriate to do a spiritual sequel to The Monster Squad where the vampire comes back from The Abyss and Rudy needs Ashley's help to end it once and for all?

Absolutely, it would be appropriate — and honestly, it could be really compelling.

Here’s why it works:
🧛‍♂️ 1. It’s Thematic Closure and Growth

Rudy has spent his whole life haunted (literally and metaphorically) by The California Incident. A sequel would allow him to face what he couldn't finish, and show how he’s grown from a teenager with a crossbow and attitude into a seasoned, emotionally scarred Hunter. Bringing Ashley back isn’t just nostalgia bait — it’s a chance to explore what surviving childhood horror does to someone over time.

Let them be broken. Let them be stronger. Let them be weird. That’s the juice.
🪦 2. You Already Have the Lore for It

In your mythos, Dracula isn’t the Dracula — he’s a Ventrue or similarly powerful vampire, possibly obsessed with final immortality (and, depending on your current canon, may have Lasombra traits or ambitions). Getting pulled into the Abyss was never a full death. It was a banishment.

The return of this vampire from the Abyss is a classic return of the repressed moment — and in your world, the Abyss has gravity. Maybe it didn't spit him out so much as sent him back with a purpose.
👧 3. Ashley Deserves Her Moment

In The Monster Squad, Phoebe is iconic but underdeveloped. Giving Ashley her moment as an adult allows you to explore who she became. Did she lean into the weirdness? Did she forget? Did she always remember but never believed it? Or maybe she’s the one who kept the faith all along, while Rudy tried to bury it.

Their reunion can be a clash — or a rekindling.
🔥 4. It Honors the Same Spirit as Those Meddling Kids

Cantero’s novel is a love letter to aging and trauma as much as it is a riff on Scooby-Doo. Your take on a Monster Squad sequel would do the same, filtered through Chronicles of Darkness grit. You’re not mocking the past — you’re reckoning with it. That’s what makes it land.
✍️ So… What Would It Look Like?

Here are a few quick angles you could take:

    Title: What Comes Back or The Thing in the Dark That Waited

    Setup: Strange murders echo the California Incident. Victims are staked, burned, or drained. Rudy investigates and finds signs he hoped he’d never see again. Then someone sends him a photograph of a girl holding a stuffed animal. Ashley's daughter. Looks just like Phoebe did.

    Complication: The vampire is back — but so is Frankenstein. Not a foe this time. He remembers.

    Theme: You can’t bury monsters. And you can’t bury what they made of you.

So yes. 100%. It would be appropriate — and powerful. If you ever want help sketching it out, I’m here for it.
