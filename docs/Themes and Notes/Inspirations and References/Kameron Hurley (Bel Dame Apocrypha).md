Cited as a model for [[What Wasn't and What Was]]'s narrative style: Hurley's Bel Dame Apocrypha (Nyx) never stops to explain its unique setting elements — it names and uses them the way its characters would, trusting the reader to keep up rather than pausing for exposition. E.g. "The bindlecat hissed. Nyx kicked it," instead of stopping to explain what a bindlecat is.

Applied directly to this book's style: don't explain every bit of the Hedge or Arcadia — let Jamie interact with setting-specific things (like the "crook-path," see [[The Huntsman]]) as normal, and let the reader catch up.
