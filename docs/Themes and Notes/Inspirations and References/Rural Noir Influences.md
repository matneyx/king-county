Comps and influences for King County's rural-noir register (isolated towns, weather as pressure, family secrets, grief tipping into the supernatural) — distinct from the general noir/hardboiled craft guide.

**Books:** *Winter's Bone* (Daniel Woodrell), *The Devil All the Time* (Donald Ray Pollock), *Crooked Letter, Crooked Letter* (Tom Franklin), *Rust & Bone* (Craig Davidson), *The Bottoms* (Joe R. Lansdale).

**Film/TV:** *Wind River* (2017), *Hell or High Water* (2016), *Out of the Furnace* (2013), *Ozark* (Netflix), *True Detective* Season 1, *Red Rock West* (1993).

**Music:** Townes Van Zandt ("Waiting Around to Die"), Gillian Welch ("Caleb Meyer" — a fun coincidental echo of [[Caleb]]'s name, nothing more), The Handsome Family ("Weightless Again"), Johnny Cash's prison songs and later albums.

**Key rural noir elements already present in King County:** isolated towns where cops know everyone but can't/won't stop anything; family secrets that outlive the people who made them; weather as pressure; truth buried deeper than bodies; grief so thick it turns supernatural.
